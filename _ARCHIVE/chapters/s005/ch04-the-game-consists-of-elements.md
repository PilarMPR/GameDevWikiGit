# s005 · Ch.4 — The Game Consists of Elements

**Book:** [[../../sources/s005-art-of-game-design|The Art of Game Design (Schell)]] · **PDF pp. 39–46**

## Chapter summary
Introduces the book's structural model of *what every game is made of*: the **elemental
tetrad** — Mechanics, Story, Aesthetics, Technology. A designer must be a "doctor" who knows
the game's anatomy *and* keep the player's experience in view at the same time ("skin and skeleton").

## Subsections

### What Are Little Games Made Of? / You're a Doctor Now
- Players have a vague sense of a game's parts; a designer must *intimately* know the anatomy —
  diagnose what's wrong, fix it, and even invent radically new "organisms" (new games) (s005, ch.4).

### The Four Basic Elements (the Elemental Tetrad)
1. **Mechanics** — the procedures and rules; the goal, allowed/forbidden actions, and what
   happens. **Mechanics are what make a game a game** (linear media lack them). Detailed in ch.10–12.
2. **Story** — the sequence of events (linear/pre-scripted or branching/emergent). Ch.15–16.
3. **Aesthetics** — how the game looks/sounds/feels; **most directly tied to the player's
   experience**. Ch.20.
4. **Technology** — *any* enabling material (paper & pencil to lasers), not just "high tech";
   the medium in which the other three occur. Ch.26.

- **Visibility gradient:** Technology = least visible to players, Aesthetics = most visible,
  Mechanics & Story in between (the diamond diagram shows visibility, **not** importance).
- **No element is more important than the others** — each powerfully influences the rest.
  Designers/artists/engineers/writers each over-value their own; *"they are all your piece."*

### 🔎 Lens #7 — The Lens of the Elemental Tetrad
- Is my design using all four element types?
- Could it improve by enhancing one or more categories?
- Are the four in **harmony, reinforcing each other** toward a common theme?

**Case study — *Space Invaders* (1978):** all four elements pull toward one fantasy (battling an
alien army): custom **technology** (motherboard enabling an advancing army); innovative,
well-balanced **mechanics** (destructible shields, bonus saucer, nearer aliens worth less,
army speeds up as you thin it, two lose-conditions); **story** changed from human soldiers →
aliens (avoids violence backlash, novel, fits the camera/aesthetic and "if they land we're
doomed" drama); **aesthetics** (3 alien designs, 2-frame march, colored plastic strips faking
color, accelerating heartbeat audio, cabinet art). Deficits in one element drove changes in another.

### Skin and Skeleton
- See past the **skin** (player experience) to the **skeleton** (elements) — but never lose the
  skin. The great challenge: *feel the experience while understanding which elements cause it,
  and why.* Focus only on skin → can't explain/improve; only on skeleton → beautiful in theory,
  horrible in play. See both at once.

### 🔎 Lens #8 — The Lens of Holographic Design
See everything at once — the four elements *and* the experience *and* their interrelations:
- What elements make the experience enjoyable?
- What elements detract?
- How can I change elements to improve the experience?
