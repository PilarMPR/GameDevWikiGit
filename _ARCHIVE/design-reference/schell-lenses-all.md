# All 100 Schell Lenses — Quick Reference

All lenses from *The Art of Game Design* (s005) in a single scannable list. For full question text, see the per-category reference sheets in [[_index]].

**Format:** #N · Name · Core question(s) · Category

---

## Experience & Design Foundations (ch.1–6)

**#1 · Essential Experience** — What experience do I want to create? What is essential to that experience? → theory
**#2 · Surprise** — What will surprise players? Does the game generate novel surprises? → player
**#3 · Fun** — What parts are fun? What parts are not fun? Why? → player
**#4 · Curiosity** — What questions does the game make the player ask? Does it reward curiosity? → player
**#5 · Endogenous Value** — Does the game give objects real value to the player? Do players feel their achievements matter? → mechanics
**#6 · Problem Solving** — What problems does the player solve? How are they discovered? → mechanics
**#7 · Elemental Tetrad** — Are all four elements (Mechanics/Story/Aesthetics/Technology) in balance? → theory
**#8 · Holographic Design** — Can every element of my game be explained in terms of the core experience? → theory
**#9 · Unification** — Does everything in my game support a single theme? → theory
**#10 · Resonance** — What is the deepest meaning behind this game? Does it resonate beyond the mechanics? → theory

## Iteration & Process (ch.7)

**#11 · Infinite Inspiration** — What is my true source of inspiration? Am I making what I want to make? → production
**#12 · Problem Statement** — Can I clearly state the problem I am trying to solve? What are the unspoken assumptions? → production
**#13 · Eight Filters** — Does the design pass all 8 filters? (Artistic, audience, novelty, commercial, stakeholder, technical, team skills, experience) → production
**#14 · Risk Mitigation** — What could go wrong? Which risks are highest impact? How can I test riskiest elements first? → production
**#15 · The Toy** — Is my game fun to play with without a goal? Does the core interaction invite manipulation? → feel

## The Player (ch.8)

**#16 · The Player** — Am I thinking about the player in everything I do? Do I understand their demographics and psychographics? → player
**#17 · Pleasure** — What pleasures does the game create? Am I delivering the intended pleasures? → player

## Player Psychology (ch.9)

**#18 · Flow** — Are goals clear? Is there steady escalating challenge? Are distractions minimal? Are feedback loops tight? → player
**#19 · Needs** — What needs does my game fulfill? Does it promise to fulfill needs and actually deliver? → player
**#20 · Judgment** — Does the game judge the player fairly? Are performance criteria clear? → player

## Core Mechanics — Space & Objects (ch.10)

**#21 · Functional Space** — What is the functional space? Discrete or continuous? How many dimensions? Are boundaries clear? → mechanics
**#22 · Dynamic State** — What are all the objects and their attributes? What is public knowledge vs. private vs. secret? → mechanics

## Core Mechanics — Actions & Rules (ch.10)

**#23 · Emergence** — How many verbs do players have? How many objects can interact? Do surprising situations arise within the rules? → mechanics
**#24 · Action** — What are the operative actions? What resultant actions emerge? Are they interesting? → mechanics
**#25 · Goals** — Are goals clear? Do short-term goals support long-term? Do player and designer goals align? → mechanics
**#26 · Rules** — Are rules complete, understandable, and interesting? Are there loopholes? → mechanics

## Core Mechanics — Skill & Chance (ch.10)

**#27 · Skill** — What skills are required? Does practice meaningfully improve them? Is the skill gap interesting? → mechanics
**#28 · Expected Value** — What are the outcomes and probabilities of each decision? Is the decision interesting? → mechanics
**#29 · Chance** — What random elements exist? Do they feel fair? Is the skill-to-chance ratio right for the audience? → mechanics

## Balance (ch.11)

**#30 · Fairness** — Is the game fair? If asymmetric, is it balanced? How do I know? → mechanics
**#31 · Challenge** — Are challenges appropriate for the audience? Do they scale? Is there challenge for all skill levels? → mechanics
**#32 · Meaningful Choices** — Are choices meaningful? Too many or too few? Are any dominated? → mechanics
**#33 · Triangularity** — Is there a safe low-reward choice and a risky high-reward choice? Is the gradient interesting? → mechanics
**#34 · Skill vs. Chance** — Is the balance appropriate? Does randomness add uncertainty or feel unfair? → mechanics
**#35 · Head and Hands** — Does the game require physical skill, mental skill, or both? Is the balance right for the audience? → mechanics
**#36 · Competition** — Does the game make players want to win? Is competition level appropriate? → mechanics
**#37 · Cooperation** — Does the game encourage cooperation? Does it emerge naturally? → mechanics
**#38 · Competition vs. Cooperation** — Is the balance between competition and cooperation appropriate? → mechanics
**#39 · Time** — How long should the game last? Does time pressure add or frustrate? → mechanics
**#40 · Reward** — What rewards exist? When do they arrive? Are they meaningful? Are variable rewards present? → mechanics
**#41 · Punishment** — Are punishments proportional? Are they helping players improve or just frustrating? → mechanics
**#42 · Simplicity/Complexity** — What are the most complex parts? Are they worth the complexity? Is complexity emergent? → mechanics
**#43 · Elegance** — Does each element serve multiple purposes? Are there elements that serve only one purpose? → mechanics
**#44 · Character** — Does the game have distinctive quirks? Are there "lovable flaws"? → theory
**#45 · Imagination** — What must the player understand? Can imagination substitute for explicit detail? → theory
**#46 · Economy** — Is the game's resource economy balanced? Are resource rates appropriate? → mechanics
**#47 · Balance** — Does the game feel balanced? Which element is most out of balance right now? → mechanics

## Puzzles (ch.12)

**#48 · Accessibility** — Is the goal immediately clear? Does the game draw players in and make them want to interact? → mechanics
**#49 · Visible Progress** — Can players see they are making progress? Are interim steps of success available? → mechanics
**#50 · Parallelism** — When stuck on one thing, do players have other things to work on? → mechanics
**#51 · Pyramid** — Does challenge start simple and build to complexity? Is the difficulty curve gradual? → mechanics
**#52 · The Puzzle** — Does the puzzle fit naturally into the world? Does solving it feel satisfying and earned? → mechanics

## Interface (ch.13)

**#53 · Control** — When players use the interface, does it do what they expect? Do players feel in control? → interface/feel
**#54 · Physical Interface** — Is the physical input well-suited to the game? Can the mapping be more direct? → feel
**#55 · Virtual Interface** — Does the virtual interface give players the information they need? Does it ever get in the way? → interface
**#56 · Transparency** — Is the interface invisible when working? Are hidden states ever surprising the player? → interface
**#57 · Feedback** — Is feedback clear, immediate, and informative for every player action? → feel/interface
**#58 · Juiciness** — Is every interaction satisfying? Are there small touches that reward incidental interaction? → feel
**#59 · Channels and Dimensions** — Am I using all available channels (visual/audio/haptic) thoughtfully? Are any overloaded? → interface
**#60 · Modes** — Are modes clear? Is it always obvious which mode the player is in? → interface

## Interest Curves & Story (ch.14–15)

**#61 · Interest Curve** — How is the experience shaped over time? Is there a hook? A rising arc? A climax? Fractal structure? → level-design
**#62 · Inherent Interest** — What is inherently interesting about this experience? What makes players curious about the next moment? → level-design
**#63 · Beauty** — Is the game world beautiful? Does the beauty serve the gameplay? → level-design
**#64 · Projection** — Does the game world invite imagination and projection? Does the player feel ownership of their character? → player/narrative

## Narrative (ch.15)

**#65 · Story Machine** — Does the game generate interesting stories by itself? Are the generated stories interesting to spectators? → narrative
**#66 · Obstacle** — Does the protagonist have a clear goal? Is the obstacle interesting in its own right? → narrative
**#67 · Simplicity and Transcendence** — Is the world simpler than real life in interesting ways? More wonderful? → narrative
**#68 · Hero's Journey** — Does the protagonist face a call, threshold crossing, trials, ordeal, and transformation? → narrative
**#69 · Weirdest Thing** — What is the weirdest thing? Is there only one? Does it make the world more interesting? → narrative
**#70 · Story** — Does the game tell a good story through play, not just cutscenes? → narrative

## Indirect Control & Space (ch.16–19)

**#71 · Freedom** — Do players have real freedom or just the feeling of freedom? Are constraints natural? → narrative/level-design
**#72 · Indirect Control** — What indirect control methods am I using? Are they working without feeling manipulative? → narrative/level-design
**#73 · Collusion** — Am I asking NPCs and environment to secretly cooperate to create good experiences? → narrative
**#74 · The World** — Is the game world interesting to explore? Is it consistent? Does it have a coherent tone? → narrative

## Characters (ch.18)

**#75 · The Avatar** — Does the avatar invite identification? Is there the right mix of "me" and "not me"? → narrative
**#76 · Character Function** — What function does each character serve? Are redundant functions present? → narrative
**#77 · Character Traits** — Are traits consistent? Are they reflected in actions, not just description? → narrative
**#78 · Interpersonal Circumplex** — Do characters cover different positions in the dominant/friendly space? → narrative
**#79 · Character Web** — What are all relationships between characters? Do they change over time? → narrative
**#80 · Status** — What is each character's status? Are status transactions dramatically interesting? → narrative
**#81 · Character Transformation** — Does the protagonist change? Is the transformation believable and earned? → narrative
**#82 · Inner Contradiction** — Does the protagonist have an inner flaw? Is resolving it part of the story? → narrative
**#83 · Nameless Quality** — Does the world have a quality beyond description — something that draws players in? → narrative

## Social & Community (ch.19–20)

**#84 · Friendship** — Does the game bring people together as friends? What do players talk about after? → player
**#85 · Expression** — Does the game let players express who they are? Are expressive options sufficient? → player
**#86 · Community** — Does the game support community formation? Give players reasons to return? → player
**#87 · Griefing** — Are there ways players can harm other players? How is antisocial behavior handled? → player
**#88 · Love** — Does the player love this game? Do I love this game? → player

## Team & Process (ch.23–28)

**#89 · The Team** — Is the team aligned? Does everyone feel ownership? Are there communication breakdowns? → production
**#90 · Documentation** — Does documentation reflect the current state? Is it being used? Is there a source of truth? → production
**#91 · Playtesting** — Are we testing with the right audience? At the right stage? Are results actually changing the design? → production
**#92 · Technology** — Is the technology right for this game? Are there technology risks? → production
**#93 · Crystal Ball** — What will gaming be like when this ships? Are we designing for the present or future? → production
**#94 · The Client** — Who is the client? What do they want vs. what do they need? → production
**#95 · The Pitch** — What is the one-sentence description? Is it compelling? Does it accurately represent the game? → production/business
**#96 · Profit** — What is the business model? Is the game going to be profitable? How do I know? → business
**#97 · Transformation** — Does this game transform the player? Is that transformation positive? → production
**#98 · Responsibility** — Am I willing to be publicly responsible for this game? Does it reflect my values? → production
**#99 · The Raven** — Does this game have heart? Is there something in it that only I could make? → production
**#100 · Your Secret Purpose** — Why am I making this game? What does it mean to me? Have I found a way to put that in? → production
