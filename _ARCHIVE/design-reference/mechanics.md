# Design Reference: Mechanics & Systems

---

## Schell's Lenses (s005)

### Space & State (ch.10)
**Lens #21 — Functional Space**
- What is the functional space of my game? Is it discrete or continuous? How many dimensions?
- What are the boundaries of the space? Are they obvious to the player?
- Is the space the same for all players? Does it change over time?

**Lens #22 — Dynamic State**
- What are the objects in the game, and what are their attributes?
- What are all the different states each object can be in?
- What state information is known to all players? What is private? What is secret?
- Would the game benefit from greater or lesser information asymmetry?

### Actions & Emergence (ch.10)
**Lens #23 — Emergence**
- How many verbs do my players have? How many objects can they interact with?
- How many objects can interact with each other?
- Can players generate surprising situations that are still governed by the rules?
- Do my rules combine to create emergent behavior I didn't anticipate? Are those behaviors desirable?

**Lens #24 — Action**
- What are the operative actions in my game?
- What are the resultant actions? Are they interesting? Do they emerge from operative actions in surprising ways?
- What would it take to add more interesting resultant actions?

### Goals & Rules (ch.10)
**Lens #25 — Goals**
- Are the goals of the game clear? Do players understand what they are trying to achieve?
- Are there short- and long-term goals? Do the short-term goals support the long-term?
- Do the player's goals align with the designer's goals?
- Is the goal achievable but not trivial? Does it feel worth achieving?

**Lens #26 — Rules**
- Are the rules complete? Are there any loopholes?
- Are the rules understandable? Can players learn them quickly?
- Do the rules lead to interesting decisions?
- Does the order of the rules matter? What is the most efficient order to present them?
- Are there any rules that cause unnecessary conflict or confusion?

### Skill & Chance (ch.10–11)
**Lens #27 — Skill**
- What skills does my game require?
- Are some players much better at these skills than others? Is the skill gap interesting or frustrating?
- Does practice improve these skills meaningfully?
- Does the game test the skills I intended?

**Lens #28 — Expected Value**
- What are all the outcomes of each decision? What are their probabilities?
- What is the expected value of each decision?
- Is the expected value of different choices balanced? Are there dominant choices?
- Is the expected value immediately obvious, or does the player need to think?

**Lens #29 — Chance**
- What random elements exist in the game? Do they feel random or do they feel fair?
- Could elements that feel random actually be made to feel more skill-based?
- Can the player control random events, or only respond to them?
- Is there a good ratio of skill to chance? Does it suit the intended audience?

### Balance (ch.11)
**Lens #30 — Fairness**
- Is my game fair to all players? Do all players feel they have a fair chance to win?
- Is my game symmetric? If asymmetric, is it balanced? How do I know?
- If the game is unfair, is that intentional (simulating a real-world asymmetry)?

**Lens #31 — Challenge**
- What are the challenges in my game? Are they appropriate for my intended audience?
- Are there enough challenges? Will players run out?
- Do the challenges increase in difficulty? At the right rate?
- Are there challenges for players of all skill levels? Can experts still find the game challenging?

**Lens #32 — Meaningful Choices**
- What choices do players make in this game?
- Are the choices meaningful? Do they matter to the outcome?
- Are there too many choices (overwhelming) or too few (trivial)?
- Are any choices dominated (one option is always best)? If so, how can I fix this?

**Lens #33 — Triangularity**
- Does my game have triangularity? Is there a safe, low-reward choice and a risky, high-reward choice?
- Is the risk-reward tradeoff interesting? Do different players prefer different options?

**Lens #34 — Skill vs. Chance**
- Should my game be more skill-based or more chance-based? Why?
- Is the current balance of skill and chance appropriate for the audience?
- Does randomness add uncertainty, or does it make the game feel unfair?

**Lens #35 — Head and Hands**
- Does my game require physical skill, mental skill, or both?
- Is the balance of physical vs. mental challenge appropriate for the audience?
- Would the game be better if it added more physical or more mental challenge?

### Multiplayer & Social Mechanics (ch.11)
**Lens #36 — Competition**
- Does my game make players want to win? What makes winning desirable?
- Is competition direct (player vs. player) or indirect (compare scores)?
- Is the competition at an appropriate level?

**Lens #37 — Cooperation**
- Does my game encourage cooperation? How is it structured?
- Does cooperation emerge naturally, or must it be forced?
- What happens when players don't cooperate? Is the game still enjoyable?

**Lens #38 — Competition vs. Cooperation**
- Does my game mix cooperation and competition? How are they balanced?
- Is the cooperation/competition balance appropriate for the experience I want?

### Time & Economy (ch.11)
**Lens #39 — Time**
- How long should my game last? Is the actual duration appropriate?
- What defines the length of my game? Can I control it more precisely?
- Does time pressure add to the experience, or does it make the game frustrating?

**Lens #40 — Reward**
- What rewards does my game provide? When?
- Do players feel rewarded appropriately for effort?
- Are there variable rewards? Fixed rewards? What are their effects?
- Could I add more interim rewards to maintain momentum?
- Are rewards meaningful to the player, or just cosmetic?

**Lens #41 — Punishment**
- What are the punishments in my game?
- Are the punishments proportional to the offense (mistake/failure)?
- Is punishment helping players improve, or just making them frustrated?
- Does punishment encourage risk aversion? Is that desirable?

**Lens #46 — Economy**
- Does my game have an economy? What is the production, distribution, and consumption of resources?
- Are all the resources balanced? Does one resource dominate all others?
- Are the resources generated at the right rate?

**Lens #47 — Balance**
- Does the game feel balanced right now? Is one player/strategy much better than another?
- What element is the most out of balance? Why?
- Am I using the right tools to measure balance?

### Elegance & Complexity (ch.11)
**Lens #42 — Simplicity/Complexity**
- What are the most complex parts of my game? Are they worth the complexity?
- What would happen if I simplified or removed the most complex element?
- Does the game have innate complexity (complexity from within the design) or emergent complexity (complexity arising from system interaction)?

**Lens #43 — Elegance**
- What are the elements of my game? How many different functions do each one serve?
- Are there elements that serve only one purpose? Could they serve more?
- Does the game have unnecessary elements that add no meaning or purpose?

### Puzzles (ch.12)
**Lens #48 — Accessibility**
- Can players immediately understand what they're supposed to do in this puzzle/game?
- Does the game act like something players have seen before? Have I drawn attention to the similarity?
- Does the game/puzzle draw players in and make them want to interact?

**Lens #49 — Visible Progress**
- Can players see that they are making progress when solving a problem?
- Are there enough interim steps of progressive success?
- Is progress visible, or hidden? Can I reveal more?

**Lens #50 — Parallelism**
- When players get stuck, do they have other things to work on?
- Is there a diversity of puzzle types so players can use different problem-solving approaches?

**Lens #51 — Pyramid**
- Does the puzzle start with simpler versions of the challenge and build to harder ones?
- Is the difficulty curve of the puzzle gradual?

**Lens #52 — The Puzzle**
- Does the puzzle fit naturally into the world?
- Does the puzzle make use of skills the player has already developed?
- Is the puzzle the right difficulty for its position in the game?
- Does solving the puzzle feel satisfying? Does it feel earned?

## Tips from Other Sources

### Adams: 5 Mechanic Types (s006, ch.14)
When designing core mechanics, ask which type your game emphasizes:
1. **Physics**: Does the physical simulation feel accurate and responsive enough?
2. **Internal economy**: Are resource production/consumption/exchange rates balanced?
3. **Progression**: Does the unlock/challenge sequence respect the player's growing skill?
4. **Tactical maneuvering**: Is the tactical decision space interesting without being overwhelming?
5. **Social interaction**: Are social mechanics positive-sum or zero-sum? Is that right for the audience?

### Adams: Intransitive balance (s006, ch.13)
- Build intransitive (rock-paper-scissors) relationships between options to prevent dominant strategies
- Every strong option should have a counter; every counter should have a counter-counter
- Test dominant strategy hypothesis: can one choice always beat all others?

### Sellers: Systemic design questions (s001, ch.5)
- What are the parts of this system? What properties and behaviors does each have?
- What are the loops? Which are reinforcing? Which are balancing?
- What emergent behaviors might arise from the interaction of parts?
- Does the player have enough feedback to form an accurate mental model?
- What is the "whole" — what experience does the full system produce?

### Macklin & Sharp: Design values (s009, ch.2)
Before each iteration, state your design values explicitly:
- What experience is this mechanic supposed to create?
- Does the current implementation serve that value?
- What would a player who cares about that value most want changed?

### Fullerton: Formal element checklist (s008, ch.3)
- Players: are all player roles clearly defined?
- Objectives: does every player always know their goal?
- Procedures: are all legal moves clear and discoverable?
- Rules: are there any ambiguous or conflicting rules?
- Resources: are resources distinct, meaningful, and appropriately scarce?
- Conflict: is the conflict interesting and proportional to the game's tone?
- Boundaries: does the player understand what is inside and outside the game?
- Outcome: is the outcome meaningful? Does it feel earned?

### Koster: Fun-as-learning checks (s016)
- Can a player who hasn't mastered the game still see what mastery would look like?
- Is there always a new pattern to learn, or has the game been "used up"?
- Does the game reveal new niceties gradually, or front-load everything?
- Would an expert player still find the game challenging in new ways?
