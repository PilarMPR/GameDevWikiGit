## What it's about
A modern, hands-on iterative design textbook organized as **Concepts → Process → Practice**.
Like Fullerton, it's playcentric, but it foregrounds **design values** (what your game is
*about*) as the thing iteration should serve (s009).

## Structure & core ideas
- **Six basic elements of play design** (ch.1): *actions, goals, rules, objects, playspace,
  players* — from six elements, "limitless play experiences." See [[../concepts/formal-elements]].
- **Basic game design tools** (ch.2): constraint, direct/indirect actions, goals, challenge,
  skill/strategy/chance/uncertainty, decision-making & feedback, abstraction, theme, storytelling.
- **The kinds of play** (ch.3): competitive, cooperative, skill-based, experience-based, chance,
  whimsical, role-playing, performative, expressive, simulation-based.
- **The player experience** (ch.4): action theory + **five layers** — *sensory, information,
  interaction, frame, purpose*. See [[../concepts/player-experience-and-ux]].
- **The iterative process** (ch.5): **conceptualize → prototype → playtest → evaluate**, a
  repeated loop; *embrace failure to succeed*. See [[../concepts/iterative-design-and-playtesting]].
- **Design values** (ch.6) — the experiential/ethical goals that guide iteration (case studies:
  *Journey*, *Desert Golfing*, *Consentacle*).
- **Documentation** (ch.7), **collaboration & teamwork** (ch.8), then the full practice arc:
  conceptualizing, **8 kinds of prototypes** (ch.10), playtesting, and shipping.

## Defining ideas
- **Design values** as the north star of iteration (s009, ch.6).
- **Prototypes are "playable questions"** — pick the prototype that answers your current
  question (s009, ch.10).
