## What it's about
A wide-ranging, humane guide to game design organized around **100+ "lenses"** — short sets
of focusing questions, each a different perspective for examining your game. The premise:
there is no single recipe; a designer improves by repeatedly looking at the game through many
lenses (s005, "Table of Lenses").

## Structure & core ideas
- **The designer** (s005, ch.1) — the skills a designer needs; the most important skill is
  **listening** (to team, audience, game, client, self); the "five kinds of listening."
- **Experience is the goal** (s005, ch.2) — "The game is not the experience." Designers create
  artifacts that *give rise to* experiences; chase the **essential experience**.
- **What is a game?** (s005, ch.3) — surveys definitions; games arise from a designer's intent.
- **The elemental tetrad** — every game is made of **Mechanics, Story, Aesthetics, and
  Technology**, which must reinforce one another (s005). Connects to [[../concepts/formal-elements]].
- **Iteration & prototyping** — risk assessment, "Eight tips for productive prototyping,"
  the rule of *fail faster*; loop until "good enough" (s005, ch.7–8). See
  [[../concepts/iterative-design-and-playtesting]].
- **Design for the player** — demographics, psychographics, projecting yourself into the
  player (s005, ch.8). Connects to [[../concepts/player-experience-and-ux]].
- Later sections span mechanics, balance, interest curves, story, world-building, characters,
  the team, and the business of games — each surfaced through lenses.
