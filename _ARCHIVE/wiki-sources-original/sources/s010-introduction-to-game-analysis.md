## What it's about
A method for **analyzing games as cultural texts** — how to study and write about an existing
game rigorously. Complements the design books: where they teach you to *build*, this teaches
you to *read* games critically (s010, ch.1).

## Structure & core ideas
- **Why & how games are "texts"** and what **textual analysis** is (ch.1).
- **Preparing the analysis** (ch.2): sourcing, *playing critically*, what "finished" means,
  interactivity & critical distance, walkthroughs, gathering information.
- **Three areas of analysis**:
  1. **Context** (ch.3) — production team, genre, technological/socio-historical/economic
     context, audience, relations to other media.
  2. **Game Overview** (ch.4) — players, rules/goals/modes, mechanics, spaces, fictional world,
     story, gameplay experience, communities.
  3. **Formal Elements** (ch.5) — rules of the world, diegetic vs extradiegetic rules,
     abstraction, **procedural rhetoric / values**, dynamics, point of view, control schemes,
     difficulty/balance, representation & identity, **levels & level design**, choice design.
- Builds toward synthesizing observations into a coherent written analysis.

## Defining ideas
- A **structured framework** (Context / Overview / Formal Elements) for disciplined analysis (s010).
- **Procedural rhetoric** — games make arguments through their rules and systems (s010, ch.5).
