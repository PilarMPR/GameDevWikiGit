## What it's about
A shared **vocabulary** for talking about *how games communicate* and *what they mean*.
The authors argue that much of the difficulty in making and discussing games comes from
imprecise language; a better vocabulary lets designers reason about craft (s002, ch.1 "Language").

## Core ideas
- **Games speak in verbs, not nouns.** What the player *does* (the verbs) is the heart of a
  game's meaning; objects exist to give verbs context (s002, ch.2 "Verbs and Objects").
  Good design creates **meaningful choices** through rules. Links [[../concepts/game-design-vocabulary]].
- **Scenes** = how verbs and objects are arranged, shaped, and paced over a play session;
  introduces **shaping & pacing**, **layering**, and **moments of inversion** (s002, ch.3).
- **Context** — visual/fictional framing teaches the player how to read the verbs (s002, ch.4).
- Part II (Clark) extends the vocabulary to **resistance/challenge, feedback, and how a
  game's communication produces experience and meaning** (s002, Part II).
- Strong stance that games are an **expressive medium** — design choices carry authorial
  meaning, not just challenge. Connects to [[../concepts/theory-of-fun]] and contrasts with
  purely systems/balance framings.
