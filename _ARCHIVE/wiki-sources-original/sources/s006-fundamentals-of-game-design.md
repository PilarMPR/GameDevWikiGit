## What it's about
A comprehensive textbook built around **player-centric design**: the designer is an advocate
for the player, defining a clear **target audience** and the **player experience** before
mechanics (s006, ch.2, ch.4). Combines a general design process with deep dives into the
craft areas and the major genres.

## Structure & core ideas
- **What is a game / video game** (ch.1) and the **design process, team roles, and design
  documents** (ch.2); introduces the structure of a video game and the anatomy of a designer.
- **Genres** (ch.3) and **Understanding Your Player** (ch.4) — incl. *VandenBerghe's Five
  Domains of Play*; warns against binary thinking about players.
- **Understanding your machine** (ch.5) and **Making money** (ch.6, payment models).
- **Game concepts** (ch.7) and **Game worlds** — purpose & dimensions of a world (ch.8).
- **Creative/expressive play** (ch.9) and **character development** (ch.10).
- **Storytelling** (ch.11) — linear vs nonlinear, the storytelling engine, dialogue trees.
- **Creating the User Experience** (ch.12) — player-centric **UI**, interaction & camera
  models, accessibility. See [[../concepts/player-experience-and-ux]].
- **Gameplay** (ch.13) — *the hierarchy of challenges*; skill, stress & absolute difficulty.
- **Core mechanics** (ch.14) — the rules/data that implement gameplay. See [[../concepts/formal-elements]].
- Followed by genre-specific design chapters.

## Defining ideas
- **Player-centric design** & the **target audience** as the anchor of every decision (s006, ch.2,4).
- **The hierarchy of challenges** as the structural backbone of gameplay (s006, ch.13).
- Clean separation of **gameplay = challenges + actions**, implemented by **core mechanics** (s006, ch.13–14).
