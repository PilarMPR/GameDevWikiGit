# Steve Swink

**Role:** Game designer, author, indie developer · **Known for:** *Game Feel*; co-founder of Enemy Airship

---

## Key ideas

- **Game feel**: the tactile, kinesthetic quality of controlling a game character — the sensation of "being" the avatar. "The input structure is the player's tactile contact with the game." (s007)
- **Three building blocks**: game feel is produced by three elements working together: real-time control (responsive input), simulated space (a world with physical properties), and polish (visual/audio/haptic embellishment). (s007)
- **Six measurable components**: Input · Response · Context · Polish · Metaphor · Rules. These are the empirically measurable dimensions of game feel — each can be tuned independently. (s007)
- **The 240ms boundary**: the player must receive a response to their input within 240ms for the game to feel real-time. Beyond this, the causal link between input and response breaks. (s007)
- **Identity extension**: the upper target of game feel design — the player stops feeling like they are controlling a character and starts feeling like they *are* the character. The controller becomes transparent. (s007)
- **Input-to-response mapping**: the designer's primary creative act in control design. The curve shape (linear, exponential), deadzone, and responsiveness all produce the character's "feel" before any content is added. (s007)

## Notable works

- *Game Feel: A Game Designer's Guide to Virtual Sensation* (2009) — the only full-length book on game feel. (s007 in this wiki)
- Key game design work at Enemy Airship and prior studios; contributed to multiple indie and mid-tier projects

## Living output

- Less public output than some peers; the book remains the primary resource
- The concepts from the book are frequently cited in GDC talks on "juice" and feel; his work influenced the genre of "game feel talks" (Purho/Jonasson's "Juice It or Lose It" is the most visible successor)

## Position in the wiki

His work is directly applicable to Hot Potato's brawler input design.

## Tensions with other thinkers

- **vs. Norman (s015)**: Norman's feedback principles operate at the UI/information level; Swink's operate at the kinesthetic/proprioceptive level. Complementary: Norman covers what information is communicated; Swink covers how interaction *feels* in the body.
- **vs. Hodent (s014)**: Hodent's cognitive science approach covers perception and memory; Swink covers the embodied, motor-learning dimension. Neither alone is sufficient for complete UI+feel design.
