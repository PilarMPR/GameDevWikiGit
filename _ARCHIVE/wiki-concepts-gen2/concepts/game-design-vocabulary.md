# Game Design Vocabulary (verbs & objects)

The argument that better **language** for game design yields better games, from
[[../sources/s002-a-game-design-vocabulary|Anthropy & Clark]] (s002).

## Core ideas
- A precise shared **vocabulary** lets designers reason about and critique craft; vague language
  is a real obstacle to good work (s002, ch.1 "Language").
- **Games speak in verbs.** What the player *does* is the seat of meaning; **objects** give the
  verbs context. Design = creating **meaningful choices** through rules (s002, ch.2).
- **Scenes** arrange verbs/objects with **shaping, pacing, layering**, and **moments of
  inversion** (s002, ch.3); **context** (visual/fictional framing) teaches the player how to
  read the verbs (s002, ch.4).
- Games are an **expressive medium** — design carries authorial meaning, not just challenge (s002).
