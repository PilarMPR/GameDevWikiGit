# Player Experience & Game UX

How the player perceives, understands, and feels a game — and how to design for that
deliberately. A bridge between **design** and **human factors**.

## Key ideas across sources
- **Player-centric design** — the designer is the player's advocate; define the **target
  audience** and intended experience first ([[../sources/s006-fundamentals-of-game-design|Adams s006]],
  ch.2,4,12). "The game is not the experience" — design the artifact that *produces* the
  experience ([[../sources/s005-art-of-game-design|Schell s005]], ch.2).
- **Layers of experience** — Macklin & Sharp's five: **sensory, information, interaction, frame,
  purpose** ([[../sources/s009-games-design-and-play|s009]], ch.4).
- **Game UX = Usability + Engage-ability** — usability removes friction; engage-ability sustains
  motivation/emotion ([[../sources/s014-the-gamers-brain|Hodent s014]]). Built on cognitive
  science (perception, attention, memory, motivation, emotion, learning).
- **Usability foundations** — affordances, signifiers, mapping, feedback, conceptual models,
  the gulfs of execution & evaluation, designing for error ([[usability-and-hcd]],
  [[../sources/s015-design-of-everyday-things|Norman s015]]).
- **Game feel** — the felt, kinesthetic layer of real-time control ([[game-feel-and-3cs]],
  [[../sources/s007-game-feel|Swink s007]]).
- The player's **mental model** (Norman's conceptual model applied to games) is what the game's
  feedback must shape — see [[fun-and-interactivity]] and [[meaningful-play]].
