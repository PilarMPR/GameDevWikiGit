# MDA Framework (Mechanics–Dynamics–Aesthetics)

The most widely cited lens in game design, from [[../sources/s011-mda-framework|Hunicke, LeBlanc & Zubek (2004)]] (s011).

## The three layers
- **Mechanics** — components at the level of data and algorithms (rules/systems).
- **Dynamics** — run-time behavior of mechanics responding to player input over time.
- **Aesthetics** — the emotional responses evoked in the player.

**Designer reads M→D→A; player experiences A→D→M.** Each is a causally linked **lens**; a small
mechanical change cascades up into dynamics and felt experience — hence **experience-first
(not feature-first) design** (s011).

## The 8 kinds of "fun" (aesthetics taxonomy)
Sensation · Fantasy · Narrative · Challenge · Fellowship · Discovery · Expression · Submission (s011).
A deliberate replacement for the vague word "fun." Compare [[theory-of-fun]] (Koster: fun = learning).
