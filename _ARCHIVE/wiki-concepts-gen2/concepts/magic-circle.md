# The Magic Circle

The idea that a game creates a **bounded space, set apart from ordinary life, within which its
own rules apply.**

## Origin and lineage
- **Huizinga** ([[../sources/s004-homo-ludens|Homo Ludens]], s004) — play occupies special
  "playgrounds… the temple, the stage, the screen… within which special rules obtain." This is
  the root of the term (s004).
- **Salen & Zimmerman** ([[../sources/s013-rules-of-play|s013]]) adopt and sharpen it into a
  design concept: the magic circle is the boundary where the artificial conflict of a game
  becomes real and meaningful for players (s013).
- **Sellers** ([[../sources/s001-advanced-game-design|s001]], ch.3) restates it: a game is a
  **nonconsequential** context with its own tokens and rules ([[game-as-system]]).

## Why it matters
Defines where/when the game's rules and **meaningful play** ([[meaningful-play]]) hold. The
player voluntarily steps inside; inside, ordinary consequences are suspended and the game's
quantifiable outcomes take over.
