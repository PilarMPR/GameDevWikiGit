# Ideation — Inspiration, Problem Statements, Subconscious (s005 Ch.6, Lenses #11–12)

The "amateurish" loop (think of an idea, try it, keep changing until good) is exactly what real designers do — ch.6-7 teach how to do it well. This chapter covers where ideas come from and how to use them productively (s005, ch.6).

## Detail

- **Lens #11 — Infinite Inspiration:** look everywhere *except* other games for inspiration. Others can copy your moves but not your inspiration. Ask: What experience from my life would I want to share? How can I capture its essence?
- **Lens #12 — Problem Statement:** design = problem solving. Before generating ideas, write a problem statement naming your goal AND constraints. Three advantages: broader creative space, clear measurement ("does this solve the problem?"), better team communication. If you have no constraints, invent some.
- **The creative subconscious:** treat it as a separate person — can't talk (communicates in imagery/emotion), impulsive, emotional, playful. Five tips: pay attention (listen to thoughts/feelings/dreams), record your ideas (frees the mind), manage its appetites, sleep (the mind sorts/files), don't push too hard.
- **Brainstorming disciplines:** quantity over quality; defer judgment; welcome wild ideas; build on others' ideas; stay visual.
