# The Experience is in the Player's Mind — Flow, Needs (s005 Ch.9, Lenses #18–20)

Experiences happen in the brain — mostly subconscious. Four mental abilities make gameplay possible: modeling, focus, imagination, empathy. Lens #18 introduces the flow channel; Lens #19 connects to Maslow's hierarchy of player needs (s005, ch.9).

## Detail

- **Modeling:** the mind deals only with simplified models of reality. Games are "pre-digested models" — relaxing because they strip away reality's complexity. The player's mental model ≡ Norman's conceptual model ≡ Sellers' player mental model loop.
- **Focus (Lens #18 — Flow):** the flow channel = sustained focus + enjoyment. Components: clear goals, no distractions, direct feedback, continuous achievable challenge. Stay between boredom (too easy) and anxiety (too hard). As skill rises, challenge must rise. Use tense-and-release to climb the channel.
- **Imagination:** the mind fills in gaps. Games don't need to show everything; the art is knowing what to show and what to leave to imagination ("the binoculars effect" — a close-up first establishes the image; imagination sustains it).
- **Empathy:** humans project decision-making capacity into perceived agents (dogs evolved expressive faces to capture our empathy). In games we project our whole decision-making self into a character — deeper than audience empathy for film characters.
- **Lens #19 — Needs (Maslow):** know which level of the hierarchy your game is promising to fulfill; verify through playtesting that it delivers.
- **Lens #20 — Judgment:** How do players judge themselves through my game? Do they feel competent, powerful, creative? Does the game make them feel good/bad about themselves?
