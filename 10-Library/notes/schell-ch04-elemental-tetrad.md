# The Elemental Tetrad — Mechanics, Story, Aesthetics, Technology (s005 Ch.4, Lenses #7–8)

Every game is made of four elements: **Mechanics** (procedures, rules, and goals — what makes a game a game), **Story** (sequence of events, linear or emergent), **Aesthetics** (sensory experience — most directly tied to the player's experience), and **Technology** (any enabling material — paper to lasers). No element is more important than the others; all must reinforce each other (s005, ch.4).

## Detail

- **Lens #7 — Elemental Tetrad:** Is my design using all four element types? Could it improve by enhancing one or more? Are they in **harmony, reinforcing each other** toward a common theme?
- **Lens #8 — Holographic Design:** think about all four elements simultaneously; "skin and skeleton" — the doctor analogy: know the anatomy intimately while keeping the player experience in view.
- **Space Invaders case study:** all four pull toward one fantasy (battling an alien army). Custom motherboard technology enabling an advancing alien army. Innovative mechanics. Simple story (invaders descend). Classic aesthetic. All four reinforce a single experience.
- **Visibility gradient:** Technology = least visible to players; Aesthetics = most visible. The gradient does NOT correlate with importance — all four are equally powerful.
- ⚠️ Schell's Aesthetics = visual/audio/haptic style. MDA's Aesthetics = emotional responses. Different uses of the same word.
