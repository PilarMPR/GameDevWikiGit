# Player Motivation — Maslow, Intrinsic/Extrinsic, SDT

Three layered frameworks for understanding why players play: **Maslow's hierarchy** (which human need is being fulfilled), **intrinsic/extrinsic motivation** (whether the reward is internal or external), and **Self-Determination Theory** (the three innate needs that sustain intrinsic engagement). The undermining effect — where extrinsic rewards erode intrinsic motivation — is the central ethical design challenge in F2P games (s014, ch.7; s005, ch.9).

## Detail

- **Maslow in games (s005, ch.9):** Most games operate at esteem (level 4 — achievement, mastery, recognition). Multiplayer games can reach belonging (level 3 — explaining their stronger pull). Creative/community games reach 3–5. A game must deliver the need it promises; failure to do so produces particularly bitter disappointment (Lens #19: Needs).
- **Intrinsic motivation:** engaging because the activity itself is rewarding — learning, exploration, creativity, flow, social connection. Durable and healthy. Koster's fun-as-learning is the cognitive account of intrinsic motivation (s016).
- **Extrinsic motivation:** engaging for an external reward (XP, points, money, recognition). Powerful for initiating behavior; produces different (and more fragile) engagement patterns.
- **The undermining effect:** adding expected, tangible extrinsic rewards to an intrinsically motivating activity can reduce intrinsic motivation. The player reframes the activity as "work for reward." Central concern in F2P design (s014, ch.7).
- **SDT (Ryan & Deci via s014):** three innate needs sustain intrinsic motivation: **Competence** (feeling effective and growing), **Autonomy** (meaningful choices, agency), **Relatedness** (connection to others or story characters). Games satisfying all three produce the strongest intrinsic engagement.
- **Variable ratio reward schedule:** most persistent engagement effect; the "slot machine" mechanism. Ethical design ensures the activity itself is rewarding; exploitative design makes the reward the only reason to continue (s014, ch.7).
