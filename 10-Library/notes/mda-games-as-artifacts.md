# Games as Artifacts — Content is Behavior

Unlike film, music, or literature, games are **artifacts rather than media**: their content is the *behavior* the system produces, not a fixed stream of assets. The string of events in a play session is unknown until the game is actually running with a player; the designer creates the possibility space, not the specific events (s011).

## Detail

- **Artifacts vs. media:** books, films, and music stream content directly to the audience. Games provide a rule-system that the player activates to produce behavior. The player co-creates the content.
- **Design implication:** you cannot design the player's experience directly — you can only design mechanics that, through dynamics, tend to produce the intended aesthetic. The causal chain is indirect.
- **Playtesting necessity:** because behavior is emergent, the designer cannot fully predict outcomes from inspection of Mechanics alone. Playtesting is the only way to observe the Dynamics and Aesthetics layers.
- **Contrast with authored content:** scripted cutscenes and linear levels are more media-like (fixed output); open-world systems and multiplayer are more artifact-like (emergent output).
