# The Game Improves Through Iteration — Rule of the Loop (s005 Ch.7, Lenses #13–15)

The heart of the design process: **iterate**. "The more times you test and improve your design, the better your game will be." Not a lens — an absolute truth. Schell introduces the spiral model (risk assessment + prototypes + looping) as the professional standard (s005, ch.7).

## Detail

- **Rule of the Loop:** iterate as many times as you can afford. Two questions: How can I make every loop count? How can I loop as fast as possible?
- **Eight Filters (Lens #13):** a finished design must pass all eight — artistic impulse, demographics, experience design, innovation, business/marketing, engineering, social/community, playtesting. Changing to pass one may break another; re-run all.
- **Lens #14 — Risk Mitigation:** identify your greatest risks early; build prototypes specifically to mitigate them. Don't prototype what you know — prototype what you're uncertain about.
- **Lens #15 — The Toy:** make the core interaction feel satisfying before adding game structure. "Build the toy first" — if it's not fun to manipulate, no amount of game structure will save it.
- **Spiral model (Boehm):** loop: basic design → identify greatest risks → build prototypes → test → refine design → repeat. Risk assessment drives what gets prototyped next.
- **Cerny's Method:** make a small, polished version of the core experience (2 "publishable levels"); pre-production ends when those are fun.
