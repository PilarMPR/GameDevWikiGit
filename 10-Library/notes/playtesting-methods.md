# Playtesting — Methods and Disciplines

Playtesting is a **learning exercise** ("what is happening and why?"), not a validation exercise ("is this good?"). It is the primary feedback mechanism for iterative design. The facilitator's cardinal rule: observe without interfering (s008; s001).

## Detail

- **Six playtest types (s009):** Internal (design team — fast but blind to new-player problems), Developer circle (precise actionable feedback), Friend/family (accessible but socially softened), Target audience (closest to real players), New player (onboarding testing), Experienced player (mid/late-game content).
- **Match playtester to stage:** rough paper prototype → developer feedback; near-final digital → target audience feedback.
- **Data types (s008):** Qualitative (think-aloud protocol, post-session interviews, observation notes), Quantitative (completion rates, attrition points, time-spent, retry rates), Behavioral (what players do vs. what they say — trust behavior over stated preference).
- **The facilitator's discipline:** do not explain anything the game doesn't explain itself (confusion = data); do not lead with pointed questions; do not defend design decisions; do not help stuck players (struggle = data). "Observe, don't explain." (Sellers, s001).
- **Evaluation:** cluster observations by design area → filter against design values → identify minimum change → look for what can be removed, not just added.
