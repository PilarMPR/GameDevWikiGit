# Monetization Models and Ethical Design

Modern monetization is inseparable from game design — the model chosen shapes player experience, design incentives, and ethical posture. The gold standard for competitive games: **gameplay equity + cosmetic monetization** (s017).

## Detail

**Revenue models (s006, s017):** Premium (one-time purchase), Subscription, F2P (IAP-based), Season pass/DLC, Battle pass, Ads.

**Pay-to-win vs. cosmetic:** Pay-to-win = purchasing gameplay advantages; destroys competitive balance and community trust. Cosmetic = visual customization with no gameplay impact; players self-select; sustainable. For competitive games, cosmetic monetization is the only viable long-term approach.

**IAP patterns:**
- **Two-currency system:** soft (earnable) + hard (purchasable) — abstracts real-money purchase; gives non-payers access to most content.
- **Price anchoring (s014):** first price seen becomes reference point (Weber-Fechner). Catalogs designed with this in mind.
- **Gacha/loot boxes:** variable ratio reinforcement (strongest engagement schedule). Ethical concern: slot-machine psychology applied to purchases; many jurisdictions moving toward regulation.

**Ethical lines (s014):** Undermining effect — aggressive extrinsic rewards erode intrinsic motivation. Loss aversion exploitation (FOMO timers). Variable ratio for purchases specifically. Ethical test: would players feel fairly treated upon reflection?
