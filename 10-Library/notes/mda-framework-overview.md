# MDA Framework — Mechanics, Dynamics, Aesthetics

MDA decomposes a game into three causally linked layers: **Mechanics** (the rules/algorithms), **Dynamics** (run-time behavior that emerges when mechanics interact with player input), and **Aesthetics** (the emotional responses evoked in the player). The framework's central claim is that these layers are not independent — a change in Mechanics propagates through Dynamics to reshape the Aesthetics the player experiences (s011).

## Detail

- **Mechanics** — components at the level of data representation and algorithms; the rules and systems encoded in the software.
- **Dynamics** — the run-time behavior of mechanics acting on player inputs and each other over time; loops, strategies, and emergent behaviors.
- **Aesthetics** — the desirable emotional responses in the player; the *felt* experience. MDA replaces the vague word "fun" with 8 specific aesthetic categories.
- Games are artifacts, not media — their content is *behavior*, not media streaming out; the string of events is unknown until the product runs (s011).
- Schell's 8 pleasures ≡ MDA's 8 aesthetics — same taxonomy, slightly different framing (s005, s011).
