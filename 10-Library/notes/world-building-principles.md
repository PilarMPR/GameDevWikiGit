# World Building — Consistency, Theme, Environmental Story

A game world contextualizes mechanics, houses narrative, motivates exploration, encodes theme, and enables player imagination. Its primary design constraint: **internal consistency**. Players accept impossible worlds (dragons exist) but are disturbed by inconsistencies within a world's own logic (s005, ch.17).

## Detail

- **Consistency as foundation (s005):** the question is not "is this realistic?" but "does this follow the rules this world has established?" Players build mental models of world-rules; violations feel like betrayal.
- **Environmental storytelling (s012):** what has happened (damage tells history), who lives here (design of spaces reveals inhabitants), what is valued (most prominent elements), what is happening now (active elements make the world feel alive independent of the player).
- **Theme drives world design (s005, ch.5):** the world should embody the game's thematic argument spatially and procedurally — not just as backdrop for story but as the space within which the theme is experienced.
- **Closed vs. open worlds (s006):** Closed = bounded, fully authored (design challenge: make it feel alive). Open = expansive with player-generated paths (design challenge: density and variety without "empty world" syndrome).
- **Biomes/regions** serve navigational orientation, tonal variety, and mechanical variety simultaneously.
