# Adams' Five Mechanic Families

Ernest Adams classifies core mechanics into five functional families based on the *type of challenge and interaction* they implement. This taxonomy is genre-diagnostic: most games are dominated by one or two family types, and the family type determines the characteristic challenge and fun (s006, ch.13–14).

## Detail

1. **Physics mechanics**: simulate physical forces and spatial relationships — collision, momentum, projectile trajectories, gravity. Dominant in: action games, platformers, physics puzzles. Challenge type: spatial reasoning, timing, precision.
2. **Internal economy mechanics**: resources are created, stored, exchanged, and destroyed. Income/expenditure loops, supply/demand, trade. Dominant in: strategy, city-builders, RPGs, F2P games. Challenge type: resource optimization, planning, trade-offs.
3. **Progression mechanics**: the game state advances, restricts, and unlocks based on criteria. Level gates, quest completion, skill trees. Dominant in: RPGs, action-adventures, metroidvanias. Challenge type: exploration, accumulation, mastery expression.
4. **Tactical maneuvering mechanics**: positioning, timing, and movement in conflict situations. Unit positioning, cover, range. Dominant in: strategy, tactics, competitive shooters. Challenge type: spatial reasoning combined with prediction of opponent behavior.
5. **Social interaction mechanics**: coordination, communication, competition, and trust between players. Voting, trading, role-playing, deception. Dominant in: multiplayer games, party games, social deductions. Challenge type: reading people, coordination, negotiation.

Each family has distinct design tools, balance methods, and player skill profiles. [[game-balance-methods]] differ per family: physics games balance by tuning forces; economy games balance by modeling exchange rates; social games balance by managing information asymmetry.
