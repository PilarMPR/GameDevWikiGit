# Game Mechanics — Six Categories and Nine Lenses (s005 Ch.10, Lenses #21–29)

Mechanics are "the interactions and relationships that remain when aesthetics, technology, and story are stripped away." Six mechanic categories with nine lenses (Lenses #21–29): Space, Objects/Attributes/States, Actions, Rules, Skill, and Chance. This is Schell's most detailed mechanics treatment (s005, ch.10).

## Detail

- **Lens #21 — Functional Space:** the abstract topology of play: discrete vs. continuous, dimensions, bounded/connected areas. The magic circle as geometry.
- **Lens #22 — Dynamic State:** objects = nouns; attributes (categories) + states (current values) = adjectives. State machines for complex behaviors. Key question: who knows what? Changing public/private info radically changes a game.
- **Lens #23 — Emergence, #24 — Action:** operative actions (base moves) vs. resultant actions (strategic moves that emerge). The ratio of resultant:operative measures emergence. Five ways to plant emergence: more verbs, verbs acting on many objects, goals achievable multiple ways, many subjects, side effects that change constraints.
- **Lens #25 — Goals:** good game goals are concrete (know when achieved), achievable, worthwhile. Consider short-term and long-term goals. Goals define the player's direction.
- **Lens #26 — Rules:** the rules create the possibility space. Rules should be: available (visible), clear (understood), fixed (not arbitrary), and sufficient (define complete game behavior).
- **Lens #27 — Skill, #28 — Expected Value, #29 — Chance:** skill vs. chance balance. Expected value = probability × outcome value. Chance creates uncertainty; must be calibrated so the game feels fair.
