# Bartle's Four Player Types

Richard Bartle's taxonomy from MUD research identifies four archetypes based on two axes: acting vs. interacting and world vs. players. The types are design lenses for audience analysis and feature prioritization — not rigid boxes for classifying people. Most players are mixtures; the taxonomy reveals *what the design must address* (s005, ch.8).

## Detail

| Type | Motivation | Card suit |
|---|---|---|
| **Achievers** | Accumulate points, items, and status; complete goals | ♦ Diamonds |
| **Explorers** | Discover the breadth of the game; understand how it works | ♠ Spades |
| **Socializers** | Relationships with other players; the social dimension | ♥ Hearts |
| **Killers** | Compete with and defeat other players; dominate | ♣ Clubs |

- **Achievers** need: clear granular goals, quantifiable progress, meaningful rewards, completionist content.
- **Explorers** need: hidden content, deep systems worth probing, lore, debug/understanding tools.
- **Socializers** need: communication tools, cooperative mechanics, social visibility.
- **Killers** need: competitive systems with clear winners, meaningful stakes, skill differentiation, leaderboards.
- **Warning:** "Bartle's taxonomies have gaps, and when misused can gloss over important nuances." (Schell, s005, ch.8). Use as a design checklist, not a classification tool.
- **Player lifecycle:** new players tend Achievers → experienced players shift to Explorers → long-term players shift to Killers or Socializers (s001, ch.4).
