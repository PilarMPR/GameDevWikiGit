# The Game is Made for a Player — Demographics, Pleasures (s005 Ch.8, Lenses #16–17)

To make a great experience, know your audience better than they know themselves. Lens #16 (The Player): stop thinking about the game, think about the player. Lens #17 (Pleasure): LeBlanc's 8 pleasures = specific vocabulary for what players seek (s005, ch.8).

## Detail

- **Empathy/projection:** know the audience through deep observation, not assumption. Walt Disney crouching to a child's eye level at Disneyland. If you were once in the audience, mine old memories; if not, study them like a cultural anthropologist.
- **Nine age groups** from infant/toddler to 50+. Younger groups divided by mental development; older by family transitions. The 13-18 (male/female interests diverge) and 25-35 (peak family formation) segments are most design-critical.
- **Gender generalizations (for large audiences, not individuals):** males tend toward mastery, competition, destruction, spatial puzzles, trial & error. Females tend toward emotion, real world, nurturing, dialog/verbal puzzles, learning by example. Never applied to individuals.
- **LeBlanc's 8 pleasures (Lens #17):** Sensation, Fantasy, Narrative, Challenge, Fellowship, Discovery, Expression, Submission. Equivalent to MDA aesthetics (s011). The vocabulary for targeting specific player experiences.
- **Bartle's four player types:** Achievers, Explorers, Socializers, Killers. Use as design checklist, not classification system.
