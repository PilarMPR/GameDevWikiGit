# MDA Feedback Dynamics — Positive and Negative Loops

Within MDA's Dynamics layer, feedback loops are the dominant mechanism. **Positive feedback** (reinforcing) amplifies an advantage — the rich get richer. **Negative feedback** (balancing) corrects toward equilibrium. The design challenge is ensuring the feedback structure in Dynamics produces the intended Aesthetics, not the opposite (s011; s001, ch.7).

## Detail

- **Positive feedback (reinforcing loop):** output amplifies the input condition. In Monopoly: owning more properties → earning more rent → buying more properties. Without a countervailing mechanism, positive feedback produces a runaway leader and early game-over for everyone else.
- **Negative feedback (balancing loop):** output reduces the gap. Harder challenges → more practice → more skill → challenge feels easier. Stabilizing; creates the flow channel.
- **The Monopoly design flaw (s011):** Monopoly's mechanics create a runaway positive feedback loop. The intended Aesthetics were Challenge and Fellowship, but the actual Aesthetics produced are boredom and resentment as the losing players wait for the inevitable. The Dynamics betrayed the Aesthetics.
- **Designing feedback:** identify what loop types your Mechanics create in the Dynamics layer *before* the intended Aesthetic is ruined. Use negative feedback loops to balance positive feedback.
