# Game Analysis — Context, Overview, Formal Elements

Fernández-Vara provides a structured method for analyzing games as cultural texts. Three areas of analysis: **Context** (production team, genre, socio-historical setting), **Game Overview** (players, rules/goals, mechanics, spaces, fictional world), and **Formal Elements** (rules, abstraction, procedural rhetoric, dynamics, difficulty, representation). The framework allows rigorous, discipline-independent game criticism (s010).

## Detail

- **Procedural rhetoric (s010, citing Bogost):** games make arguments through their rules and mechanics, not just their representations. A game where aggression always leads to victory makes a claim about aggression. This is meaning-making at the formal level, above narrative.
- **Game analysis vs. game design:** these methods are complementary. Analysis teaches you to read games; design teaches you to build them. Designers who can analyze are better designers.
- **Three lenses required:** formal-only analysis misses experiential and cultural dimensions. Cultural-only misses what makes games games (the mechanics, rules, and procedures).
