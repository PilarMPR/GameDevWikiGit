# The Designer Creates an Experience — Lens #1 (s005 Ch.2)

The designer's true goal is **not the game but the experience the game produces**. Games are artifacts — worthless unless played. Lens #1 (Essential Experience): stop thinking about the game; think about the player's experience. Ask: What experience do I want? What is essential to it? How can my game capture that essence? (s005, ch.2).

## Detail

- **"The game is not the experience."** The game enables the experience but is not the experience itself. Like building a ship in a bottle — the output is never directly seen by the designer; it's an unsharable experience in another mind.
- **Essential experience:** you don't replicate a real experience — you capture its essence. Wii Sports baseball captured the essential experience of a swing; dropped innings and base-stealing. Knowing the essence tells you which elements you can safely change and which you cannot.
- **Three practical tools for understanding experience:** Psychology (mechanisms of mind, both behaviorist and phenomenological), Anthropology (fieldwork: live with your subjects), Design xenophilia (pull good ideas from every design discipline).
- **Introspection has perils:** false conclusions about reality (acceptable in game design — we care about what feels true), and failure to generalize to others (the real danger). Solution: observe your own experience AND others', model the difference.
- **Dissect your feelings:** put concrete words to abstract feelings to communicate the target experience to your team.
