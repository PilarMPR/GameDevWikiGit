# Schell's 8 Pleasures ≡ MDA's 8 Aesthetics

Two of the most-cited "kinds of fun" lists are **functionally equivalent**. LeBlanc's 8 **aesthetics** (formalized in MDA, s011) and Schell's discussion of player **pleasures** (s005) both replace the vague word "fun" with a directed vocabulary of specific emotional targets. They name nearly the same set of experiences from two directions — design-first (MDA) and lens-first (Schell) (s005, s011).

## Detail

- **Why it matters:** both lists exist to make "make it fun" actionable. Instead of one undifferentiated goal, you target *named* responses — and different mechanics serve different ones. State which aesthetics/pleasures a game is for, then derive mechanics backward from them (s011).
- **The MDA 8 aesthetics (s011):** Sensation, Fantasy, Narrative, Challenge, Fellowship, Discovery, Expression, Submission.
- **Schell's framing (s005):** Schell catalogs player pleasures using nearly identical categories (anticipation, completion, surprise, triumph over adversity, etc.) and folds them into lenses such as Lens #17 (The Lens of Pleasure). The overlap with LeBlanc is close enough to treat the two as one toolkit.

| MDA aesthetic (s011) | Schell pleasure framing (s005) |
|---|---|
| Sensation | Sense-pleasure: visual/audio/haptic delight |
| Fantasy | Make-believe; inhabiting another world |
| Narrative | The unfolding dramatic story |
| Challenge | Triumph over adversity; mastery |
| Fellowship | The social frame; cooperation and shared experience |
| Discovery | Curiosity, exploration, surprise |
| Expression | Self-expression and creation |
| Submission | The pleasure of the magic circle as pastime; flow-as-relaxation |

- **Caveat:** neither list is exhaustive or scientific (Schell, s005, is explicit that pleasure taxonomies are starting points, not a complete map). Nicole Lazzaro's **4 Keys** (Hard/Easy/Serious/People Fun) is a third overlapping cut. Use them as complementary checklists for what experience the design is reaching for.
