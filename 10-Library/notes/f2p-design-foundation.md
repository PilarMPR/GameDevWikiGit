# Free-to-Play Design — Fun First, Then Monetize

The core F2P principle: **fun first**. Without a compelling core experience, no economy, retention loop, or monetization survives. F2P is not monetization bolted onto a game — it is a design philosophy that shapes every aspect from moment-to-moment feel through economy to social systems. Explicitly builds on MDA (s011) (s017).

## Detail

- **Core stance:** the free experience must be genuinely good. A game not inherently engaging cannot be made engaging through rewards, FOMO, or payment pressure.
- **The 3Cs as foundation:** Character (avatar physicality), Camera (world framing), Controls (input responsiveness). These must be nailed before any system above them. Builds on Swink's game feel framework (s007).
- **Four-stage arc:** Core Game Design → Economy Design → Retention Loops → Monetization. Each stage rests on the one below.
- **Onboarding is critical:** F2P players have invested zero money; the retention decision happens in the first session. Must deliver fun within 60 seconds; introduce systems progressively; provide early win; give a visible hook before session ends.
- **Undermining effect risk:** aggressive extrinsic rewards erode intrinsic motivation. Players who feel they're "working for rewards" instead of playing will leave (Hodent, s014).
- **Loop architecture:** short core loops (seconds–minutes) → medium loops (daily/session) → long loops (seasonal/monthly). Players who complete a loop and see nothing to do next leave.
