# s016 · A Theory of Fun (Koster)

**TL;DR** — A single thesis, illustrated: **fun is the act of mastering a pattern — fun is just another word for learning.** Games are engines that deliver learning in a compressed, safe, pleasurable form. When the pattern is mastered, the game is "used up" and becomes boring (s016).
**Provenance caveats** — Illustrated essay; sparse machine-readable TOC. Summarized from canonical content.

## Key ideas

- **The brain craves patterns.** "Fun" is the feedback of the brain successfully learning/chunking a pattern (s016).
- Too easy = boring (pattern already known); too hard = frustrating (pattern unlearnable) — fun lives in the learnable middle. Anticipates the flow channel.
- **Games teach** — they are fundamentally educational; the formal system being learned matters more than the content.
- Distinguishes *fun* from aesthetic appreciation, social status, storytelling — those can accompany a game but aren't the game's "fun."
- Games are a legitimate **expressive medium** capable of carrying meaning like other arts.

> ⚠️ Tension with s001: Sellers says a game need not be fun if interactive. Koster would say sustained engagement *is* ongoing learning. Compare [theory-of-fun-koster](../notes/fun-as-learning-koster.md) with [fun-as-engagement-sellers](../notes/fun-engagement-debate.md).
