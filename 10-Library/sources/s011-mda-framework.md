# s011 · MDA Framework (Hunicke, LeBlanc & Zubek)

**TL;DR** — The single most-cited framework in game design. MDA decomposes a game into three causally linked layers (Mechanics → Dynamics → Aesthetics) and insists designer and player approach them from **opposite directions**: M→D→A and A→D→M respectively. Design experience-first, not feature-first (s011).
**Provenance caveats** — Read in full (5-page conference paper, AAAI Workshop 2004).

## The framework

- **Mechanics** — components at the level of data representation and algorithms (rules/systems).
- **Dynamics** — run-time behavior of mechanics acting on player inputs over time.
- **Aesthetics** — the desirable **emotional responses** evoked in the player.

## The 8 aesthetics (a directed vocabulary replacing "fun")

1. Sensation · 2. Fantasy · 3. Narrative · 4. Challenge · 5. Fellowship · 6. Discovery · 7. Expression · 8. Submission (s011).

## Key claims

- **Games as artifacts, not media** — their content is *behavior*, not media streaming; the string of events is unknown until the product runs (s011).
- **Asymmetry** — the designer reads M→D→A; the player experiences A→D→M. Adding features doesn't guarantee experience.
- **Positive vs negative feedback**: monopoly as a design flaw — positive feedback produces wrong aesthetics for Challenge+Fellowship intent.
- Schell's 8 pleasures ≡ MDA's 8 aesthetics (same taxonomy, different framing).
