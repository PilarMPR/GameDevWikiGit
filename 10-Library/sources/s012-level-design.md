# s012 · Level Design (Kremers)

**TL;DR** — A broad, theory-forward treatment of level design covering teaching the player, emotional feedback, environments, story, and gameplay construction. Emphasizes levels as teaching apparatus and emotional orchestration tools, distinct from s003's practitioner-first approach (s012).
**Provenance caveats** — Author identified from title page: Rudolf Kremers. Previously logged as unattributed (metadata not in PDF); corrected 2026-06-01.

## Structure (six parts)

- **I. Introduction** — game design vs level design; teaching mechanisms; goals & hierarchies; structure & methodology; toward a shared grammar (ch.1–6).
- **II. Emotional Feedback Systems** — reward systems, immersion, negative emotions (ch.7–9). Distinctive emphasis: levels deliberately orchestrate emotion.
- **III. Game Environments** — visual experiences, audio design (ch.10–11).
- **IV. Game Stories** — story & narrative (ch.12).
- **V. Designing Gameplay** — world building, puzzles, AI, challenge (ch.13–16).

## Key ideas

- Levels as a **teaching apparatus** — introducing and reinforcing mechanics (s012, ch.2).
- **Emotional feedback systems** (reward / immersion / negative emotion) as a core design lens (s012, Part II).
- Push toward a **shared grammar** for the level design discipline (s012, ch.6).
