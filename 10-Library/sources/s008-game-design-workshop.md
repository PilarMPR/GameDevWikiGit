# s008 · Game Design Workshop (Fullerton)

**TL;DR** — The canonical playcentric, exercise-driven game design textbook. Centers the player experience and an iterative prototype → playtest → evaluate loop; you design by making and testing playable artifacts, not by writing specs. Includes "Designer Perspective" interviews (LeBlanc, American McGee, Sandy Petersen, etc.) (s008).
**Provenance caveats** — Verified TOC + chapter conclusions + designer-perspective interviews.

## Key ideas

- **Formal elements** (ch.3): **players, objectives, procedures, rules, resources, conflict, boundaries, outcome** — the structural skeleton every game shares.
- **Dramatic elements** (ch.4): challenge, play, premise, character, story, world-building.
- **System dynamics** — games as systems of objects/properties/behaviors/relationships.
- **Playtesting as the core discipline** — design quality is discovered through play; prototypes are questions (s008).
- **Formal + dramatic + dynamic** elements as three lenses on a game.
