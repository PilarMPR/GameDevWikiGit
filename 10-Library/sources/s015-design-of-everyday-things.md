# s015 · The Design of Everyday Things (Norman)

**TL;DR** — The seminal work on human-centered design (HCD) and usability. Norman argues that most "user error" is really design error — good design makes the right action obvious, the wrong action hard, and gives clear feedback. The conceptual vocabulary of usability that game UX builds on (s015).
**Provenance caveats** — Design/HCI, not game-specific. Included because game UX (s014) and player-centric design (s006) build directly on it.

## Core vocabulary

- **Affordances** — what an object lets you do; **signifiers** — perceivable cues that communicate where/how to act.
- **Mapping** — relationship between controls and effects; good mapping is natural/spatial.
- **Feedback** — immediate, informative response to action.
- **Conceptual models** — the user's mental model; the **system image** must convey the right model.
- **Constraints** — physical/logical/cultural limits guiding correct use.
- **Seven stages of action** and **Gulf of Execution** (how to act) & **Gulf of Evaluation** (what happened) — design bridges both (s015).
- **Human error**: slips vs mistakes; design for error.
