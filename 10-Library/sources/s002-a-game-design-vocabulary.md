# s002 · A Game Design Vocabulary (Anthropy & Clark)

**TL;DR** — A shared vocabulary for talking about *how games communicate* and *what they mean*. Argues that games speak in verbs (what the player does) not nouns, and that meaning flows from rules creating meaningful choices (s002, ch.1–2).
**Provenance caveats** — Summarized from verified TOC (Part I) + canonical content.

## Key ideas

- **Games speak in verbs.** What the player *does* is the heart of meaning; objects give verbs context (s002, ch.2).
- **Scenes** = how verbs/objects are arranged, shaped, and paced (s002, ch.3). Introduces shaping & pacing, layering, moments of inversion.
- **Context** — visual/fictional framing teaches the player how to read the verbs (s002, ch.4).
- Strong stance: games are an **expressive medium** where design choices carry authorial meaning.
