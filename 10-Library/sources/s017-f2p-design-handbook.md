# s017 · Ultimate F2P Design Handbook (Genesis)

**TL;DR** — A practitioner's playbook for free-to-play game design, drawing on ~10 years of experience. Part 1 covers the core design foundation of fun — the bedrock that economy, retention, and monetization later depend on (s017, Conclusion).
**Provenance caveats** — Industry blog (Medium article series), not peer-reviewed. **Only Part 1 of 4** is present (Core Game Design). Parts 2–4 (Economy, Retention, Monetization) are referenced but not included. Author "Genesis" is a pseudonym.

## Key ideas (Part 1)

- **Foundation of fun first** — "without it, no economy, retention loop, or monetization strategy can survive" (s017). Core question: *how do we make an experience so compelling players return again and again?*
- **3Cs** — **Character, Camera, Control** — the moment-to-moment feel layer that underpins everything else.
- **Engaging systems**, smooth **onboarding**, clear **feedback loops**, **progression**, and **accessibility** as the design pillars (s017).
- Explicitly builds on the MDA framework (s011) and player-centric design.
- Framed for the **F2P live-service** context: retention/monetization (later parts) sit on top of core design.
