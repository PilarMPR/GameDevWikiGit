# s009 · Games, Design and Play (Macklin & Sharp)

**TL;DR** — A modern hands-on iterative design textbook organized as Concepts → Process → Practice. Like Fullerton, it's playcentric, but foregrounds **design values** (what your game is *about*) as the thing iteration should serve (s009).
**Provenance caveats** — Verified TOC + chapter summaries.

## Key ideas

- **Six basic elements of play design** (ch.1): *actions, goals, rules, objects, playspace, players* — "from six elements, limitless play experiences" (s009, ch.1).
- **Five layers of player experience** (ch.4): sensory, information, interaction, frame, purpose.
- **Iterative process** (ch.5): conceptualize → prototype → playtest → evaluate; *embrace failure to succeed*.
- **Design values** (ch.6) — the experiential/ethical goals that guide iteration; the north star.
- **8 kinds of prototypes** — each prototype answers a specific design question (s009, ch.10).
- Macklin's contribution: "Prototypes are playable questions."
