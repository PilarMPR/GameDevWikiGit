# s005 · The Art of Game Design (Schell)

**TL;DR** — A wide-ranging, humane guide to game design organized around 100+ "lenses" — short sets of focusing questions, each a different perspective for examining your game. The premise: there is no single recipe; a designer improves by repeatedly looking at the game through many lenses (s005, "Table of Lenses").
**Provenance caveats** — Deep chapter-by-chapter pages at [[wiki/chapters/s005/_index]] (ch.1–10 done, ch.11–33 pending).

## Structure & key ideas

- **The designer** (ch.1) — the most important skill is **listening** (to team, audience, game, client, self).
- **Experience is the goal** (ch.2) — "The game is not the experience." Designers create artifacts that *give rise to* experiences; chase the **essential experience**.
- **The elemental tetrad** — every game is made of **Mechanics, Story, Aesthetics, and Technology**, which must reinforce one another.
- **Iteration & prototyping** — risk assessment, *fail faster*; loop until "good enough" (ch.7–8).
- **Design for the player** — demographics, psychographics, projecting yourself into the player (ch.8).
- Later sections: mechanics, balance, interest curves, story, world-building, characters, team, business.
