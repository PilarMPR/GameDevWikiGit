# s014 · The Gamer's Brain (Hodent)

**TL;DR** — Applies cognitive science and UX to game development. Core argument: the brain is "biased, emotional, irrational, and yet extremely complex," so designers must understand how players perceive, attend, remember, learn, and feel — and guard against their own biases — to build engaging, usable games (s014, ch.1–2).
**Provenance caveats** — This file is a **condensed study-notes/summary edition** (Bookey-style chapter summaries), not the complete CRC Press original. Treat claims as faithful to Hodent's thesis but cite at summarized-chapter level.

## Key ideas

- **Game UX = Usability + Engage-ability.** Usability removes friction; engage-ability sustains motivation and emotion (s014).
- Cognitive mechanisms as design constraints: perception, attention, memory, motivation, emotion, learning.
- **A UX mindset must be org-wide** — not a siloed team; buy-in across design, engineering, marketing (s014, ch.17).
- **Data-driven, hypothesis-led iteration**; early UX-issue detection is far cheaper.
- Builds on Norman (s015) — affordances, signifiers, mapping, feedback as game design tools.
