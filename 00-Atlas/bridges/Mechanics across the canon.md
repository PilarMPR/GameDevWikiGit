# 🔗 Mechanics Across the Canon

The strongest cross-source convergence in game design theory: every major source has a way of decomposing "what a game is made of" at the formal level. These are all descriptions of the same structural layer.

## The core agreement

All sources converge: **players perform actions (verbs/procedures) on objects/resources, under rules, toward goals/outcomes, within boundaries.** This is what every game shares.

## Source-by-source taxonomy

| Source | Their vocabulary | Equivalent to |
|---|---|---|
| Schell (s005) | Elemental Tetrad: **Mechanics**, Story, Aesthetics, Technology | The Mechanics element |
| Schell (s005) | Six mechanic categories: Space, Objects, Actions, Rules, Skill, Chance | The internal structure |
| MDA (s011) | **Mechanics** layer = data representations and algorithms | = Schell Mechanics |
| Fullerton (s008) | 8 formal elements: players, objectives, procedures, rules, resources, conflict, boundaries, outcome | = the Mechanics layer |
| Macklin & Sharp (s009) | 6 elements: actions, goals, rules, objects, playspace, players | = Fullerton simplified |
| Adams (s006) | 5 mechanic families: physics, economy, progression, tactical, social | Type-based taxonomy |
| Anthropy & Clark (s002) | Verbs + Objects | Player-action focused |
| Salen & Zimmerman (s013) | Core mechanic = the essential repeated action | The central procedure |
| Sellers (s001) | Structures (tokens/rules = nouns/verbs), functional loops, wholes | Systems framing |
