# 🔁 Iteration & Playtesting — The Method

The strongest methodological consensus across all design sources: you cannot design a great game by thinking; you can only do it by making and watching people play. Iteration is the method; playtesting is how you learn.

## Four framings of the same method

| Source | Their framing | Unique contribution |
|---|---|---|
| Fullerton (s008) | Playcentric cycle: conceptualize → prototype → playtest → evaluate → revise | Playcentric mindset; physical prototypes first |
| Schell (s005, ch.7) | Rule of the Loop; spiral model; 8 prototyping tips | "Fail faster"; risk mitigation; Cerny's Method |
| Macklin & Sharp (s009) | Design values anchor iteration; prototypes are "playable questions"; 8 kinds | Design values as evaluation criteria |
| Sellers (s001) | The designer's loop = one of the four principal loops | Systems framing; observe, don't explain |

## Common principles across all four

1. Prototype as early as possible
2. Make the prototype answering the most dangerous question first
3. Watch players — don't explain or defend
4. Design values/experience goals are what you evaluate against
5. More iterations = better game (no exceptions)
