## State of the field — the consolidated picture

- **Games are rule-based systems activated by a player.** Convergence across s001, s004, s006, s008, s011, s013 — the strongest consensus claim in the wiki. Operationalized via formal elements (s006, s008, s009), MDA's Mechanics layer (s011), and Sellers' systems anatomy (s001).
- **MDA is the dominant shared model.** Mechanics → Dynamics → Aesthetics (s011) is cited in concept pages, design-reference sheets, game analyses, and the Hot Potato GDD. It functions as the field's common analytical language even where authors use different vocabularies.
- **The core method is iterative and player-centered.** Prototype → playtest → evaluate is endorsed by s001, s005, s008, s009, s017; operationalized as the Cerny Method, Fullerton's 6-step playcentric cycle, and Schell's Rule of the Loop. No credible counter-tradition exists in the corpus.
- **"Fun" remains the field's live conceptual dispute.** Three positions coexist without resolution: fun = pattern learning (s016); discard "fun" for 8 aesthetics (s011); engagement ≠ fun (s001). Synthesized in [[wiki/concepts/theory/theory-of-fun]] and [[wiki/syntheses/frameworks-comparison]] as competing definitions converging on *sustained engagement*.
- **Cognitive science is the underexplored load-bearing layer.** s014 (Hodent) and s015 (Norman) supply the mechanism behind experience design — perception, memory, attention, gulfs of execution/evaluation — but are thinner in the corpus than design-theory sources. s014 is a summary/notes edition, not full original.
- **Narrative design has no dedicated source.** Coverage is assembled from s005 ch.15, s006 ch.11, s012 ch.12, and s013 (embedded/emergent narrative). The auteur/narrative-first tradition the thesis identifies as a potential falsifier of the systems framing is entirely absent.
