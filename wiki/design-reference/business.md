# Design Reference: Business & Monetization

---

## Schell's Lenses (s005)

**Lens #95 — The Pitch**
- What is the one sentence that describes this game?
- Why would someone want to play it? What is the hook?
- Does the pitch accurately represent what the game actually is?
- Is the pitch compelling to the target audience?

**Lens #96 — Profit**
- What is the business model for this game?
- Is the game going to be profitable? How do I know?
- Are the costs and revenues understood and realistic?
- Does the business model align with the experience I want to create?

## Tips from Other Sources

### F2P Design: Core principles (s017)

**Fun first checklist:**
- Is the core gameplay compelling without any monetization?
- If all premium content were removed, would players still enjoy the game?
- Does the monetization reinforce the core loop, or does it interrupt it?

**3Cs quality check:**
- **Character**: does the player character move and respond satisfyingly?
- **Camera**: does the camera support readable, enjoyable play at all times?
- **Controls**: are controls responsive, learnable, and natural for the platform?

If any 3C fails, fix it before adding any economy or monetization.

**Onboarding F2P checklist:**
- Does the player experience something genuinely fun within the first 60 seconds?
- Are new systems introduced one at a time, not front-loaded?
- Does the player earn an early win in the first session?
- Does the session end with something compelling to return for?

**Retention loop checklist:**
- Is there a reason to return tomorrow? (Daily mission, pending reward, incomplete content)
- Is there a reason to return next week? (Weekly event, seasonal content, friend activity)
- Is there a reason to return in a month? (Season progression, long-term goals)

### Hodent: Ethical monetization evaluation (s014, ch.7)

**The fair reflection test:**
Ask: if players understood every mechanism in the monetization system, would they feel they were treated fairly?
- If yes: the design is ethical
- If no: identify which mechanism feels unfair and remove or change it

**Reward schedule audit:**
- Are variable ratio rewards (loot boxes, gacha) used? Are they the primary engagement driver?
- Could the same engagement be achieved through a more predictable reward structure?
- Is the activity itself intrinsically rewarding, or only the reward structure?

**Undermining effect check:**
- For each type of intrinsically motivating activity in your game: are you monetizing it directly?
- Would players who pay to progress through content they enjoy feel that play became work?
- Are extrinsic rewards (XP, currency, items) adding to the experience, or replacing it?

**Loss aversion audit:**
- Are any mechanics designed to make players feel they will *lose* something if they don't pay?
- Is time-limited content (FOMO) making players feel coerced rather than excited?
- Are expiring offers designed to create genuine urgency or manufactured anxiety?

### Adams: Revenue model selection (s006, ch.6)
For each candidate business model, answer:
- Does this model align with the audience's expectations and willingness to pay?
- Does this model create appropriate design incentives? (Pay-to-win creates incentive to degrade free play)
- Does this model allow enough design freedom to make the game you want to make?
- Does this model fit the platform's conventions and player expectations?

### F2P: 4-stage architecture checklist (s017)
Before launch, verify each stage is designed:
1. **Core game**: is it fun without any economy or monetization? (Non-negotiable)
2. **Economy**: are the soft/hard currency flows balanced? Is there no pay-to-win for competitive content?
3. **Retention**: are daily, weekly, and seasonal loops defined and tested?
4. **Monetization**: are IAP prices anchored correctly? Are value propositions clear?

### Pricing principles
- First price seen becomes the anchor — choose it deliberately
- Bundles should feel like genuine value, not manufactured urgency
- The cheapest tier should be a real entry point, not a trap
- The most expensive tier should be clearly for superfans, not a manipulation

### Community health
- Does the monetization affect social dynamics? (Pay-to-win destroys community trust)
- Are there mechanisms for the community to compete on equal terms?
- Does the monetization model give the developer incentive to keep the game healthy?
