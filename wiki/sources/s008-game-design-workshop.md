## What it's about
The canonical **playcentric, exercise-driven** game design textbook. Centers the **player
experience** and an **iterative prototype → playtest → evaluate** loop; you design by making
and testing playable artifacts, not by writing specs (s008). Interleaved "Designer
Perspective" interviews (American McGee, Sandy Petersen, Lorne Lanning, Marc LeBlanc, etc.).

## Structure & core ideas
- **The role of the game designer** and the playcentric mindset (advocate for the player).
- **Formal elements** (ch.3): **players, objectives, procedures, rules, resources, conflict,
  boundaries, outcome** — the structural skeleton every game shares. See [[../concepts/formal-elements]].
- **Dramatic elements** (ch.4): **challenge, play, premise, character, story, world-building**;
  debunks "the two great myths of interactive storytelling."
- **System dynamics** — games as systems of objects/properties/behaviors/relationships
  (echoes [[../concepts/systems-thinking]]).
- **Working with system dynamics, prototyping, playtesting, and iteration** — the heart of the
  method: prototypes are questions; playtest early and often. See
  [[../concepts/iterative-design-and-playtesting]].
- Later: functionality/completeness/balance/fun testing, and team/production/business.

## Defining ideas
- **Formal + dramatic + dynamic** elements as the three lenses on a game (s008).
- **Playtesting as the core discipline** — design quality is discovered through play (s008).
