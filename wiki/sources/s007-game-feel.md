## What it's about
The first systematic treatment of **"game feel"** — the tactile, kinesthetic sensation of
controlling a virtual object in real time (the "juice" that makes *Mario* or a good racing
game feel good). Swink turns an intuitive, ineffable quality into something analyzable and
designable (s007).

## Core definition & framework
- **Game feel = real-time control of virtual objects in a simulated space, with interactions
  emphasized by polish** (s007).
- **Three building blocks**:
  1. **Real-time control** — continuous, responsive input within the perception-action loop.
  2. **Simulated space** — spatial physics/objects the player navigates.
  3. **Polish** — effects (sound, particles, screen shake, animation) that emphasize and sell
     interactions. (s007)
- **Metrics** — game feel can be *measured*: input response, position/velocity/acceleration
  curves, etc. Swink dissects real games' control metrics (s007).
- Grounds it in **perceptual psychology** — proprioception, the body schema, and how players
  extend their sense of self into the avatar (s007). Connects to [[../concepts/player-experience-and-ux]].
