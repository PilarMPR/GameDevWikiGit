## What it's about
A practitioner's playbook for **free-to-play (F2P) game design**, drawing on ~10 years of
experience. Part 1 covers the **core design foundation of fun** — the bedrock that economy,
retention, and monetization later depend on (s017, Conclusion).

## Core ideas (Part 1)
- **Foundation of fun first** — "without it, no economy, retention loop, or monetization
  strategy can survive" (s017). The driving question: *how do we make an experience so
  compelling players return again and again?*
- **Fun, balanced mechanics**, then intuitive **3Cs** — **Character, Camera, Control** — the
  moment-to-moment feel layer. Connects to [[../concepts/game-feel-and-3cs]].
- **Engaging systems**, smooth **onboarding**, clear **feedback loops**, **progression**, and
  **accessibility** (s017).
- Explicitly builds on the **[[s011-mda-framework|MDA framework]]** and player-centric design
  (cited in references) (s017).
- Framed for the **F2P live-service** context where retention/monetization (later parts) sit
  on top of core design. See [[../concepts/free-to-play-design]].
