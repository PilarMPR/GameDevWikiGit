## What it's about
A rigorous theoretical foundation for game design built on **systems** and **meaning**. Its
central concept is **meaningful play**, and it analyzes games through three families of
**schemas**: **RULES** (games as formal systems), **PLAY** (games as experiential systems),
and **CULTURE** (games as cultural systems) (s013).

## Two defining definitions (s013)
- **Game:** "A game is a system in which players engage in an **artificial conflict**, defined
  by **rules**, that results in a **quantifiable outcome**."
- **Meaningful play** emerges when the relationships between player **action** and system
  **outcome** are both **discernable** and **integrated** into the larger context of the game.

## Selected structure (the three schemas)
- **RULES** — games as systems: information theory systems (ch.16), systems of information
  (perfect/imperfect/"enchanted" info, ch.17), **cybernetic systems** (feedback loops, dynamic
  difficulty, ch.18), **game theory** systems (ch.19), systems of **conflict** (ch.20),
  and **breaking the rules** (player types: standard/dedicated/unsportsmanlike; degenerate
  strategies; cheats & spoil-sports, ch.21).
- **PLAY** — **defining play** ("free movement within a more rigid structure"; transformative
  play, ch.22), the **play of experience** (the *core mechanic*, ch.23), the **play of
  pleasure** (autotelic play, ch.24), meaning, narrative, simulation, social & cultural play.
- **CULTURE** — games as cultural rhetoric, open culture, and resistance.
- Includes original **commissioned games** as worked examples.
