## What it's about
Applies **cognitive science and UX** to game development. Core argument: the brain is
"biased, emotional, irrational, and yet extremely complex," so designers must understand how
players actually **perceive, attend, remember, learn, and feel** — and guard against their own
biases — to build engaging, usable games (s014, ch.1–2).

## Core ideas
- **Understand the brain's mechanisms** — perception, attention, memory, motivation, emotion,
  and learning — as the foundation of design decisions (s014).
- **Game UX = Usability + "Engage-ability."** Usability removes friction (the player can do
  what they intend); engage-ability sustains motivation and emotion. See
  [[../concepts/player-experience-and-ux]].
- **A UX mindset must be org-wide** — not a siloed UX team; buy-in across design, engineering,
  marketing, and business intelligence (s014, ch.17 "UX Strategy").
- **Data-driven, hypothesis-led iteration**; early UX-issue detection is far cheaper (s014).
