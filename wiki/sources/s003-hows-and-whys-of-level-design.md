## What it's about
A practitioner's primer on **level design** — the craft of building the spaces and moment-to-
moment experiences players move through. Argues level design is a young, often misunderstood
profession whose quality can make or break a game, yet has little foundational reference
material (s003, Preface "Why This Book?").

## Core ideas
- Distinguishes **game design vs. level design**: level designers work within given mechanics
  to shape pacing, flow, difficulty, and mood (s003).
- Emphasizes **gameplay-first** layout — readability, guiding the player, sightlines, flow, and
  pacing of tension/release — before aesthetics (s003).
- **Mood and atmosphere** via lighting, composition, and audio as deliberate emotional tools (s003).
- A practical, opinionated process for planning, blocking out, and iterating on a level (s003).
