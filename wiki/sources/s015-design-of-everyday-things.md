## What it's about
The seminal work on **human-centered design (HCD)** and usability. Norman argues that most
"user error" is really **design error** — good design makes the right action obvious and the
wrong action hard, and gives clear feedback (s015).

## Core concepts (the vocabulary of usability)
- **Affordances** — what an object lets you do; **signifiers** — perceivable cues that
  communicate where/how to act (s015).
- **Mapping** — the relationship between controls and effects; good mapping is natural/spatial.
- **Feedback** — immediate, informative response to action.
- **Conceptual models** — the user's mental model of how something works; the **system image**
  must convey the right model.
- **Constraints** — physical/logical/cultural limits that guide correct use.
- **The seven stages of action** and the **Gulf of Execution** (knowing how to act) & **Gulf of
  Evaluation** (knowing what happened) — design bridges both gulfs (s015).
- **Human error**: slips vs mistakes; design for error (s015).
