# Tracy Fullerton

**Role:** Game designer, academic, author · **Institution:** USC Games (University of Southern California) — she founded and directs the program · **Studio:** USC Game Innovation Lab

---

## Key ideas

- **Playcentric design**: design should be centered entirely on the player's experience, assessed through continuous playtesting with real players. The designer's job is to be an advocate for the player. (s008)
- **Formal + dramatic + dynamic elements**: games have three layers of components — formal elements (the structural skeleton: players, procedures, rules, etc.), dramatic elements (the experiential layer: challenge, play, premise, character, story), and dynamic elements (the play in motion). (s008)
- **Iterative design as the method**: conceptualize → prototype → playtest → evaluate → repeat. Physical prototypes first. No full production until the core is proven. (s008, ch.1, ch.9)
- **The designer as student of the player**: the designer cannot know if the design works without watching real players play. Playtesting is not validation; it is learning. (s008)
- **Playtesting is not QA**: usability testing (can they play?) and playtesting (are they having the right experience?) are different activities with different protocols. (s008, ch.9)

## Notable works

- *Game Design Workshop: A Playcentric Approach to Creating Innovative Games* (2004, 4th ed. 2018) — the most widely used game design textbook at the undergraduate level. (s008 in this wiki)
- *Walden, a game* (2016) — an art game simulating Henry David Thoreau's year at Walden Pond; demonstrates her commitment to games as meaningful expressive artifacts
- USC Games — the program she built is one of the top game design programs in the US; its alumni include prominent independent and AAA designers

## Living output

- Game design workshops and syllabi publicly available through USC Games and the textbook
- Speaks at GDC and academic conferences on playcentric design methodology
- The *Walden, a game* project continues to be developed and studied as educational/art software

## Tensions with other thinkers

- **vs. Schell (s005)**: Both emphasize iteration and playtesting; Fullerton is more systematic (formal elements, clear process structure) while Schell is more craft-intuitive (lenses, listening). Complementary — Fullerton for process, Schell for perspective.
- **vs. Macklin & Sharp (s009)**: Both emphasize design values and playtesting; Macklin & Sharp use "design values" as the explicit anchor where Fullerton uses the playcentric experience goal. Close cousins with slightly different framing.
- **vs. MDA (s011)**: Fullerton's "dramatic elements" partially overlap with MDA's Aesthetics but she treats them separately from the formal skeleton. MDA doesn't separate the structural from the experiential; Fullerton does. Both are useful.
