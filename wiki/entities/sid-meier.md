# Sid Meier

**Role:** Game designer, programmer, co-founder of Firaxis Games · **Known for:** Civilization series, Pirates!, Railroad Tycoon, Covert Action · **Studio:** Firaxis Games (Maryland)

---

## Key ideas

- **"A game is a series of interesting decisions"** — the most widely cited one-liner in game design. Every mechanic, every level, every interface element should be evaluated against this standard: does it produce interesting decisions? (cited in s001 Sellers and widely throughout the field)
  - *Interesting* means: the player has enough information to make a meaningful choice, the options are non-trivially different, and the outcome matters.
  - *Series* means: decisions are linked over time — past choices constrain future choices.
  - This definition is *not* about fun, challenge, or aesthetics — it is about the information environment that surrounds choices.
- **Strategic depth from simple rules**: the Civilization design philosophy demonstrates that deep strategic complexity emerges from a small set of simple, composable rules. The individual mechanics (move, attack, build, research) are simple; their interaction produces strategic depth. This is the systems thinking argument (Sellers s001) applied in practice.
- **Player-authored stories**: Meier's games are designed so that each player's game generates a unique story. The mechanics are a story machine (Schell Lens #65) — no two Civilization games are the same narrative.
- **Accessibility and depth**: Meier's games are generally accessible to new players (simple early decisions) but maintain deep late-game complexity (many interacting systems). This is the same "low floor, high ceiling" principle required by party brawlers and educational games.

## Notable works

- **Pirates!** (1987, MicroProse) — revolutionary open-world design for its time; player agency in an otherwise static game landscape
- **Railroad Tycoon** (1990, MicroProse) — laid out the "Tycoon" simulation genre
- **Civilization** (1991, MicroProse) — one of the most influential games ever made; the 4X genre; generations of designers cite it as formative
- **Civilization II–VI** (1996–2016, Firaxis) — the series has been continuously updated, demonstrating sustained design excellence over 35 years
- **Alpha Centauri** (1999, Firaxis) — widely considered the deepest narrative game in the strategy genre; strong thematic coherence
- *Sid Meier's Memoir!* (2020) — autobiography with design philosophy

## Living output

- *Sid Meier's Memoir!* (2020) — available and widely read; contains candid design reflections
- Firaxis continues shipping games; Meier remains involved
- Multiple interviews and GDC talks across his career

## Tensions with other thinkers

- **vs. Anthropy (s002)**: Meier's games are about decisions and systems; Anthropy argues for verbs and expressive meaning. Meier's decisions are instrumental (win); Anthropy's verbs can be expressive (say something about the world). Complementary for different design goals.
- **vs. MDA (s011)**: Meier's "interesting decisions" maps most directly to the Challenge aesthetic, but Civilization also serves Discovery, Narrative (player-authored), and Submission (just one more turn). MDA is more complete; Meier's definition is more useful as a single diagnostic question.
