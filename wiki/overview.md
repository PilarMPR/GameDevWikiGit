## State of knowledge
**17 sources ingested** — a solid slice of the game-design canon. The field converges on five
load-bearing ideas:
1. **Games are rule-based [[concepts/systems-thinking|systems]]** that become games only when a
   **player** engages them ([[concepts/game-as-system]], [[concepts/magic-circle]], [[concepts/meaningful-play]]).
2. **Structure:** the [[concepts/formal-elements|formal elements]] and the
   [[concepts/mda-framework|MDA]] model (Mechanics → Dynamics → Aesthetics).
3. **Experience is the goal:** [[concepts/player-experience-and-ux|player-centric UX]],
   [[concepts/usability-and-hcd|usability]], and [[concepts/game-feel-and-3cs|game feel]].
4. **Process:** [[concepts/iterative-design-and-playtesting|iterate — prototype, playtest, evaluate]]
   around a clear vision ([[concepts/systemic-design-process]]).
5. **Specializations:** [[concepts/level-design]], [[concepts/game-balance]],
   [[concepts/free-to-play-design]], and [[concepts/game-analysis]].

**Best-known live debate:** what "fun" actually is — see [[concepts/theory-of-fun]].
