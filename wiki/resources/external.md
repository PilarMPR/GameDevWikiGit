# External Resources

Curated links to the game design knowledge that lives outside the 17 books in this wiki. Each entry is annotated with why it's here and which concept page it extends.

This is not exhaustive — it is filtered for quality, relevance, and free availability.

---

## GDC Talks

All freely available on the [GDC YouTube channel](https://www.youtube.com/@GDC) unless noted.

### Game Feel & Controls
- **"Juice It or Lose It"** — Jonasson & Purho (2012) · Game mechanics juice at the micro level; every interaction can feel alive or dead. The talk that put "juiciness" in the design vocabulary. → [[../concepts/feel-and-controls/game-feel]], [[../concepts/feel-and-controls/feedback-systems]]
- **"The Art of Screenshake"** — Jan Willem Nijman (2013) · How to make your game's feedback feel physically real. Practical, visual. → [[../concepts/feel-and-controls/game-feel]]
- **"Game Feel: Why Your Wombat Feels Like a Beached Whale"** — Petri Purho (2012) · Precursor to Swink's book; real-time control and identity extension concepts. → [[../concepts/feel-and-controls/input-and-controls]]

### Mechanics & Balance
- **"Cursed Problems in Game Design"** — Alex Hutchinson (2019) · When design pillars directly conflict with each other; how to identify and resolve structural tensions. → [[../concepts/mechanics/emergence]], [[../concepts/mechanics/game-balance]]
- **"Rules of the Game: Five Techniques from Quite Inventive Designers"** — Stone Librande (2010) · Pattern-based mechanic design from successful designers. → [[../concepts/mechanics/core-mechanics]]
- **"Into the Breach: Design Secrets"** — Subset Games (2018) · Extreme clarity in information design; puzzle-perfect mechanic communication. → [[../concepts/mechanics/puzzles]], [[../concepts/interface/ui-design]]
- **"Magic: The Gathering's Design Lessons"** — Mark Rosewater's talks (multiple years on GDC Vault) · The deepest thinking on game design available; accessible through his [Blogatog](https://blog.markrosewater.tumblr.com) (free) and "Drive to Work" podcast. → [[../concepts/mechanics/core-mechanics]], [[../concepts/mechanics/game-balance]]

### MDA & Theory
- **"Dynamics: The State of the Art"** — Clint Hocking (2010) · The designer of Far Cry 2 on MDA's Dynamics layer; why designers must reason explicitly about emergent behavior. → [[../concepts/theory/mda-framework]]
- **"Classic Game Postmortem" series** (GDC Vault, various) · Lead designers retrospecting on landmark games. Highly variable quality; the Doom, Quake, Dune II, and System Shock entries are exceptional. → [[../concepts/production/iterative-design]]

### Story & Narrative
- **"Narrative Legos"** — Matthew Weise (2011) · Building modular, reusable narrative systems. → [[../concepts/narrative/story-and-games]]
- **"Building Non-Linear Narratives in Horizon: Zero Dawn"** — Blake Rebouche (2017) · How a fully systemic open-world game delivers authored story. → [[../concepts/narrative/world-building]]

### Level Design
- **"Level Design in a Day"** — multiple speakers (various GDC years) · Comprehensive overviews of level design practice across genres. → [[../concepts/level-design/level-design]], [[../concepts/level-design/spatial-design]]
- **"How Does Mirror's Edge Catalyst Do Parkour Level Design?"** — Tobias Dahl (2017) · Spatial flow and teaching through environment. → [[../concepts/level-design/spatial-design]]

### Player Psychology & UX
- **"The Gamer's Brain: How Neuroscience and UX Can Impact Design"** — Celia Hodent (multiple years) · The living companion to s014; her latest research beyond the book. → [[../concepts/player/player-psychology]]
- **"Designing for Disability"** — Ian Hamilton (2017, various follow-ups) · Accessibility as a design value, not a bolt-on. → [[../concepts/interface/usability-and-hcd]]

### Production & Team
- **"The Making of God of War"** — Cory Barlog (2019) · Single unified design vision applied across a large team; pre-production and pivoting. → [[../concepts/production/documentation]], [[../concepts/production/team-and-culture]]
- **"How to Make Better Friends (In Games)"** — Damion Schubert (2014) · Social systems design; why people play together and how to design for it. → [[../concepts/mechanics/multiplayer-systems]], [[../concepts/player/player-types]]

### Party Games & Brawlers
- **"Designing Jackbox Party Games"** — Jeremy Braddock, et al. (multiple GDC years) · The Jackbox team on what makes party games work. → [[../genres/party-brawler]]
- **"Masahiro Sakurai on Creating Games"** — Sakurai (YouTube series, 2022–) · Direct from the creator of Smash Bros; 200+ short videos on game design, directly applicable to brawler design. **Highest signal-to-noise ratio of any free game design resource.** → [[../genres/party-brawler]], [[../analyses/smash-bros]]

### F2P & Monetization
- **"Free to Play: Economies in Practice"** — various (GDC Vault) · Economy design and monetization ethics from practitioners. → [[../concepts/business/free-to-play-design]], [[../concepts/business/monetization]]

## Academic Papers

Freely available or widely accessible.

- **"MDA: A Formal Approach to Game Design and Game Research"** — Hunicke, LeBlanc, Zubek (2004) · The original MDA paper; 5 pages; free online. → [[../concepts/theory/mda-framework]]
- **"Hearts, Clubs, Diamonds, Spades: Players Who Suit MUDs"** — Richard Bartle (1996) · The original player taxonomy paper. → [[../concepts/player/player-types]]
- **"I Have No Words & I Must Design"** — Greg Costikyan (1994) · A seminal paper on what makes a game. → [[../concepts/theory/formal-elements]]
- **DiGRA proceedings** (digra.org) · Annual academic game studies conference; papers on design, culture, and analysis. Variable quality; useful for educational games research.
- **Foundations of Digital Games proceedings** (fdg.org) · More technically-focused than DiGRA; better for formal game design research.

## Blogs & Ongoing Writing

- **Raph Koster's site** (raphkoster.com) · Regular posts extending Theory of Fun theory; current takes on game design and the industry. Essential if s016 resonated. → [[../concepts/theory/theory-of-fun]]
- **Lostgarden (Dan Cook)** (lostgarden.homestead.com) · Long-form essays on game design; "Steambirds: Survival" is a masterclass on iterative design. "Game Design Debt" is required reading for indie development. → [[../concepts/production/iterative-design]]
- **Gamasutra / Game Developer Magazine** (gamedeveloper.com) · "Postmortems" section specifically — designers writing what went right/wrong on shipped games. The postmortems section is the closest thing to a living practitioner record.
- **Designer Notes** (designernotes.net) · Soren Johnson's podcast interviewing leading designers about specific games and decisions. Long-form; exceptional signal.
- **Game Maker's Toolkit (Mark Brown)** (YouTube: @GameMakerToolkit) · Short video essays analyzing specific game mechanics with clear theoretical grounding. The best free mechanics analysis resource available. → Links to nearly every concept page in this wiki depending on the episode.
- **Extra Credits** (older archive) · Bite-sized game design theory; the older episodes (~2012–2016) are particularly useful for foundations.
- **Heterodox Game Design** (Renaud Bédard) · Technical and design essays; particularly good on procedural and emergent content.

## For Educational Games Specifically

### Organisations & Resources
- **Games for Change** (gamesforchange.org) · Annual festival; resources on impact games; grant opportunities
- **ISTE** (iste.org) · International Society for Technology in Education; edgames standards and resources
- **GlassLab / Institute of Play** · Research on learning games (some resources archived; search for their reports)
- **E-Line Media** · Commercial studio making educational games; their public talks on sustainable edgames business models are valuable
- **Filament Games** · Long-running educational game studio; blog has practical B2B edgame design insights

### Design-Specific
- **"Why Educational Games Fail"** — Kurt Squire (various) · The canonical analysis of why most edgames don't work, and what the successful ones do differently
- **"Learning to Love Educational Games"** (various authors) · GDC-adjacent talks on the design compromises and opportunities specific to the genre → [[../genres/educational-games]]

### B2B Specifics
- SCORM and xAPI documentation (ADL Initiative, adlnet.gov) — the technical standards your B2B clients will require for LMS integration
- Common Sense Media's game review criteria — understanding how your games will be assessed by school decision-makers
