# Working Thesis

_The evolving synthesis. I revise this whenever a source shifts the overall picture.
Every claim cites its source id(s)._

**Topic:** Game development (design-focused) · **Sources:** 17

**Thesis (one line):** Game design is a young but coherent discipline whose canon converges on a
few load-bearing ideas — games are **rule-based systems** that produce **experience** through
the **player's interaction**, and good design is the **iterative, player-centered** craft of
shaping that experience — while genuinely disagreeing about the vocabulary for "fun" and about
how much to formalize the process.

## The shape of the field (how the 17 sources relate)
1. **Foundations — what play/games *are*.** Huizinga's play and the **[[../concepts/magic-circle]]**
   (s004) → Salen & Zimmerman's **[[../concepts/meaningful-play]]** and games-as-systems (s013) →
   Sellers' explicit **[[../concepts/systems-thinking|systems approach]]** (s001). Strong agreement:
   a game is a bounded, rule-governed **system** that only becomes a game when a **player** engages it.
2. **Structure — what games are *made of*.** Wide convergence on **[[../concepts/formal-elements]]**
   (Fullerton s008, Macklin & Sharp s009, Adams s006, Schell's tetrad s005, Anthropy & Clark's
   verbs s002) and on **[[../concepts/mda-framework|MDA]]** (s011) as the dominant shared model:
   Mechanics → Dynamics → Aesthetics.
3. **Experience — what games are *for*.** The target is the **player's experience**
   ([[../concepts/player-experience-and-ux]]), built on usability/HCD (Norman s015), cognitive
   science (Hodent s014), and **[[../concepts/game-feel-and-3cs|game feel]]** (Swink s007).
4. **Process — how you *make* them.** Near-unanimous: **[[../concepts/iterative-design-and-playtesting|
   iterate — prototype, playtest, evaluate]]** (Fullerton s008, Macklin & Sharp s009, Schell s005,
   Sellers s001), anchored by a clear **vision/design values** ([[../concepts/systemic-design-process]]).
5. **Specializations.** [[../concepts/level-design]] (s003, s012), [[../concepts/game-balance]] (s001),
   [[../concepts/free-to-play-design]] (s017), and [[../concepts/game-analysis]] (s010, the "reading"
   counterpart to design).

## Strongest evidence / consensus
- **A game = a rule-based system + a player who interacts with it** — held by s001, s004, s006,
  s008, s011, s013 in different words.
- **Iteration + playtesting is the core method** — s001, s005, s008, s009, s017.
- **Design for the player's experience, not features** — s005, s006, s011, s014, s015.

## Tensions / contradictions
- **What is "fun"?** Koster: fun = **learning** (s016). MDA: discard "fun" for **8 aesthetics**
  (s011). Sellers: a game **need not be fun** if interactive/engaging (s001). → Reconciled as
  competing *definitions* converging on **sustained engagement**; tracked in [[../concepts/theory-of-fun]].
- **How much to formalize?** Systems/formal-model camp (s001, s013, s011) vs. craft/lens/humanist
  camp (s005 Schell's lenses, s002 expressive vocabulary). Not a factual conflict — different
  temperaments; most authors use both.
- **Provenance caveats:** s014 is a **summary/notes edition** (not full original); s012's author
  is **unattributed** in the file; s017 is a **blog** with only Part 1 of 4 present.

## What would change my mind
- A serious design tradition that rejects the systems/experience framing and ships great games
  on a purely **authored/narrative** or **auteur** model.
- Evidence the formal frameworks (MDA, systems) are **descriptive but not generative** — good
  for analysis, weak at actually producing better games.
