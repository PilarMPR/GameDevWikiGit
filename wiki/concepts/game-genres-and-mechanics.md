# Game Genres, Gameplay & Core Mechanics

How the craft books structure **what the player does** — gameplay, challenges, and the
mechanics that implement them.

## Gameplay as challenges + actions (Adams, s006)
- **Gameplay = the challenges posed + the actions the player takes** to overcome them; structured
  by **the hierarchy of challenges** (s006, ch.13).
- Implemented by **core mechanics** — the rules and data that make gameplay work (s006, ch.14).
- Organized by **genre**, with genre-specific design patterns (s006, ch.3 + later chapters).

## The "core mechanic" across sources
- **Salen & Zimmerman** ([[../sources/s013-rules-of-play|s013]], ch.23): the **core mechanic** is
  the essential repeated action at the heart of the experience.
- **Macklin & Sharp** (s009, ch.2): tools — constraint, direct/indirect **actions**, goals,
  challenge, skill/strategy/chance/uncertainty, decision-making & feedback, abstraction.
- **Anthropy & Clark** (s002): the mechanic is a **verb**.
