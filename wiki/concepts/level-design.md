# Level Design

The craft of shaping the **spaces and moment-to-moment sequences** players move through —
distinct from (but downstream of) game design. Covered by two dedicated sources here.

## Core ideas
- **Game design vs. level design** — level designers work *within* given mechanics to shape
  flow, pacing, difficulty, and mood ([[../sources/s003-hows-and-whys-of-level-design|Hourences s003]];
  [[../sources/s012-level-design|s012]], ch.1).
- **Levels teach** — a level introduces and reinforces mechanics; *teaching mechanisms* are a
  primary job of level design (s012, ch.2).
- **Goals, hierarchies, structure & methodology** — a deliberate process for planning and
  building levels (s012, ch.3–4; s003).
- **Gameplay-first layout** — readability, guiding the player, sightlines, flow, pacing of
  tension/release before aesthetics (s003).
- **Emotional feedback systems** — levels orchestrate emotion via **reward systems, immersion,
  and negative emotions** (s012, Part II).
- **Mood/atmosphere** via lighting, composition, and audio (s003; s012 Part III).
- A young field still reaching for a **shared grammar** (s012, ch.6; s003 Preface).
