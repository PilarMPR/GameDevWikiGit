# Meaningful Play

The central design goal proposed by [[../sources/s013-rules-of-play|Salen & Zimmerman]] (s013).

## Definition
**Meaningful play** emerges when the relationship between player **action** and system
**outcome** is both **discernable** (the player can perceive the result) and **integrated**
(the result matters to the larger game over time) (s013).

Paired with their definition of a **game**: *"a system in which players engage in an artificial
conflict, defined by rules, that results in a quantifiable outcome"* (s013).

## Connections
- **Discernable + integrated feedback** is the same concern as **clear feedback** in usability
  ([[usability-and-hcd]], Norman s015) and **feedback loops** in F2P design (s017).
- It operationalizes, at the experience level, the **interactive loop** between game model and
  player mental model ([[the-four-principal-loops]], s001) and MDA **dynamics**
  ([[mda-framework]], s011).
- Relates to **meaning as expression** in [[../sources/s002-a-game-design-vocabulary|Anthropy &
  Clark]] (s002) and to games as **procedural rhetoric** in [[../sources/s010-introduction-to-game-analysis|Fernández-Vara]] (s010).
