## Sources
- [[wiki/sources/s001-advanced-game-design]] — *Advanced Game Design: A Systems Approach*, Sellers (2018). Games as systems.
- [[wiki/sources/s002-a-game-design-vocabulary]] — *A Game Design Vocabulary*, Anthropy & Clark (2014). Verbs, objects, meaning.
- [[wiki/sources/s003-hows-and-whys-of-level-design]] — *The Hows and Whys of Level Design*, Hourences (2008). Level-design craft.
- [[wiki/sources/s004-homo-ludens]] — *Homo Ludens*, Huizinga (1938). The play-element of culture; origin of the magic circle.
- [[wiki/sources/s005-art-of-game-design]] — *The Art of Game Design: A Book of Lenses*, Schell (2008). 100+ lenses.
- [[wiki/sources/s006-fundamentals-of-game-design]] — *Fundamentals of Game Design 3e*, Adams (2014). Player-centric textbook.
- [[wiki/sources/s007-game-feel]] — *Game Feel*, Swink (2009). Real-time control & virtual sensation.
- [[wiki/sources/s008-game-design-workshop]] — *Game Design Workshop*, Fullerton (2008). Playcentric, iterative; formal/dramatic elements.
- [[wiki/sources/s009-games-design-and-play]] — *Games, Design and Play*, Macklin & Sharp (2016). Iterative design; design values.
- [[wiki/sources/s010-introduction-to-game-analysis]] — *Introduction to Game Analysis 3e*, Fernández-Vara. Analyzing games as texts.
- [[wiki/sources/s011-mda-framework]] — *MDA*, Hunicke/LeBlanc/Zubek (2004). Mechanics–Dynamics–Aesthetics.
- [[wiki/sources/s012-level-design]] — *Level Design* (author unattributed in file). Teaching, emotion, environments, gameplay.
- [[wiki/sources/s013-rules-of-play]] — *Rules of Play*, Salen & Zimmerman (2003). Meaningful play; rules/play/culture.
- [[wiki/sources/s014-the-gamers-brain]] — *The Gamer's Brain*, Hodent (2017) — **summary/notes edition**. Cognitive science + game UX.
- [[wiki/sources/s015-design-of-everyday-things]] — *The Design of Everyday Things*, Norman (2013). Usability / HCD foundations.
- [[wiki/sources/s016-theory-of-fun]] — *A Theory of Fun*, Koster (2013). Fun = learning patterns.
- [[wiki/sources/s017-f2p-design-handbook]] — *Ultimate F2P Handbook (Pt 1/4)*, "Genesis" (Medium). Core F2P design.
- [[10-Library/sources/s018-juice-it-or-lose-it]] — *Juice It or Lose It*, Jonasson & Purho (GDC Europe 2012). Maximum output for minimum input; juice taxonomy; game feel as feedback density.
- [[10-Library/sources/s019-the-door-problem]] — *The Door Problem*, Liz England (blog 2014). Designer's cross-cutting role; one object, every discipline; successful design is invisible.
- [[10-Library/sources/s020-designing-celeste]] — *Level Design Workshop: Designing Celeste*, Thorson & Berry (GDC 2018). One-mechanic-per-room; tiered difficulty; Assist Mode; instant respawn as flow tool.
- [[10-Library/sources/s021-30-things-i-hate-about-your-pitch]] — *30 Things I Hate About Your Game Pitch*, Brian Upton (GDC 2017). The two foundation questions; design pillars vs. pitch hooks; 30 failure modes.
- [[10-Library/sources/s022-into-the-breach-postmortem]] — *Into the Breach Design Postmortem*, Matthew Davis (GDC 2019). Perfect information; tension without RNG; Power Grid health inversion; multi-priority decisions.
- [[10-Library/sources/s023-spelunky-dna-roguelike]] — *Spelunky and the DNA of a Roguelike*, Derek Yu (synthesized GDC 2011/2021). Symmetry rule as emergence engine; structured randomness; spiky difficulty; permadeath as world-building.
- [[10-Library/sources/s024-shovel-knight-designing-by-accident]] — *Shovel Knight: Designing by Accident*, Yacht Club Games (synthesized 2014-2015). NES-constraint philosophy; constraint-as-filter; designing by accident; accidental design coherence.
- [[10-Library/sources/s025-thief-dark-project-postmortem]] — *Classic Game Postmortem: Thief: The Dark Project*, Smith & Stellmach (GDC 2016). Stealth inversion; AI crisis and three-week rewrite; Dark Object System (ECS precursor); Light Gem; scenario vs. puzzle design.
- [[10-Library/sources/s026-game-programming-algorithms-and-techniques]] — *Game Programming Algorithms and Techniques*, Madhav (2014). The wiki's first programming textbook. Game loop + delta time, 2D/3D graphics, input, sound, physics/collision, cameras, A* pathfinding, FSM AI, networking (client prediction/lockstep), UI. Bridges design intent to technical implementation.
- [[10-Library/sources/s027-level-up-scott-rogers]] — *Level Up! The Guide to Great Video Game Design (2e)*, Rogers (2014). Practitioner handbook: Three Cs (Character/Camera/Controls), Six-Step GDD, level design as Disneyland model, combat systems, enemy design, boss battles, power-ups, monetization.
- [[10-Library/sources/s028-players-making-decisions-hiwiller]] — *Players Making Decisions*, Hiwiller (2016). Decision theory + game design: anatomy of a choice, flow as design directive, MDA extension, feedback loops, formal game theory (Nash equilibria), cognitive biases, reward schedules, probability/simulation tools.
- [[10-Library/sources/s029-game-programming-patterns-nystrom]] — *Game Programming Patterns*, Nystrom (2014). Software architecture for games: Game Loop, Update Method, Component (ECS foundations), Event Queue, State/FSM, Singleton critique, Data Locality, Object Pool. Free at gameprogrammingpatterns.com.
- [[10-Library/sources/s030-game-programming-in-cpp-madhav]] — *Game Programming in C++*, Madhav (2018). Project-based 3D engine from scratch: Actor-Component model, OpenGL pipeline, skeletal animation, FMOD audio, collision detection, UI, level serialization. Sequel to s026.
- [[10-Library/sources/s031-multiplayer-game-programming]] — *Multiplayer Game Programming*, Glazer & Madhav (2016). Networked game architecture: TCP/IP stack, UDP sockets, object serialization/replication, client-server vs. P2P topologies, latency mitigation (client prediction/server rewind), Steamworks SDK, cloud hosting.
- [[10-Library/sources/s032-writing-interactive-music-sweet]] — *Writing Interactive Music for Video Games*, Sweet (2014). Wiki's first audio source. Horizontal resequencing, vertical remixing, stingers, transitions, the Composer's Toolbox, MIDI/virtual instruments, game audio history, middleware (FMOD/Wwise), business of scoring.
- [[10-Library/sources/s033-intro-game-design-prototyping-3e]] — *Introduction to Game Design, Prototyping, and Development (3e)*, Bond (2023). Comprehensive design+dev textbook (1,443 pp). Introduces the Layered Tetrad (Inscribed/Dynamic/Cultural layers wrapping Schell's Elemental Tetrad); integrates paper prototyping, C#/Unity tutorials, and agile mentality in one arc.
- [[10-Library/sources/s034-introduction-to-game-systems-design]] — *Introduction to Game Systems Design*, Gazaway (2022). Spreadsheet-first systems design textbook. Attributes, range balancing, data fulcrums, exponential growth curves, macrosystems, player engagement, probability as a design tool.
- [[10-Library/sources/s035-penner-easing-tweening]] — *Motion, Tweening, and Easing* (Ch.7 of *Programming Macromedia Flash MX*), Penner (2002). Canonical easing equation taxonomy (linear through bounce); defines the `(t, b, c, d)` convention used in every major game/UI animation library.
- [[10-Library/sources/s036-agile-game-development-keith]] — *Agile Game Development: Build, Play, Repeat (2e)*, Keith (2021). Scrum/Kanban adapted for game teams. Minimum Viable Game, user stories, Agile Design/Art/QA disciplines, live game operations, scaling Agile. Cross-links to iterative-design-process synthesis.
- [[10-Library/sources/s037-the-game-producers-handbook]] — *The Game Producer's Handbook*, Irish (2005). Definitive producer role reference. ~30 producer responsibilities, job description templates, scheduling formulas, risk management register, QA pipeline, financial management, marketing support.
- [[10-Library/sources/s038-3d-user-interfaces]] — *3D User Interfaces: Theory and Practice (2e)*, LaViola et al. (2017). Academic VR/AR interaction reference. Interaction technique taxonomy: selection/manipulation metaphors, travel (steering/teleport/redirected walking), system control; human factors (perception, cognition, ergonomics); design strategies and evaluation.
- [[10-Library/sources/s039-unreal-engine-vr-cookbook]] — *Unreal Engine VR Cookbook*, McCaffrey (2017). UE4 Blueprint recipes for VR: HMD setup (Rift/Vive/Gear VR), trace interaction, teleportation, UMG-in-VR, IK, motion controller interaction, locomotion taxonomy, VR optimization (Forward Rendering, Instanced Stereo, Hidden Area Mesh). Companion to s038.
- [[10-Library/sources/s040-unity-game-dev-24-hours]] — *Sams Teach Yourself Unity Game Development in 24 Hours*, Geig (2022). Beginner Unity handbook: 24 hours of hands-on lessons, 4 complete games, full editor workflow (scripting, physics, 2D, animation, audio, mobile, deployment).
- [[10-Library/sources/s041-roblox-game-dev-24-hours]] — *Roblox Game Development in 24 Hours: The Official Guide*, Johnson et al. (2021). Official Roblox dev textbook: Lua scripting, Studio tools, multiplayer client-server model, monetization (Robux/DevEx), player acquisition, educational relevance.
- [[10-Library/sources/s042-practical-augmented-reality]] — *Practical Augmented Reality*, Aukstakalnis (2017). Hardware/human-factors survey of AR/VR: HMD types (optical/video see-through), depth cues, tracking sensors, haptics, applications (gaming/medicine/defense/education), human factors (motion sickness, vergence-accommodation).
- [[10-Library/sources/s043-augmented-reality-pearson]] — *Augmented Reality: Principles and Practice*, Schmalstieg & Höllerer (2016). Academic AR textbook: computer vision (SLAM, marker tracking, natural features), display taxonomy, calibration, visual coherence, interaction, software architecture. 527 pp.
- [[10-Library/sources/s044-pragmatic-programmer-ch4]] — *The Pragmatic Programmer ch.4: Pragmatic Paranoia* (excerpt), Thomas & Hunt (2020). Design by Contract (preconditions/postconditions/invariants), crash early, assertive programming (leave assertions in production), resource balancing (allocator must deallocate).
- [[10-Library/sources/s045-game-development-and-production-bethke]] — *Game Development and Production*, Bethke (2003). Production methodology textbook: project triangle, requirements capture (UML use cases), GDD/TDD structure, Gantt/PERT scheduling, feature creep control, QA pipeline, outsourcing (audio/art/VO/writing), shipping process.
- [[10-Library/sources/s046-digital-vfx-and-compositing]] — *Digital Visual Effects and Compositing*, Gress (2015). VFX pipeline and techniques: film/video primer, compositing fundamentals, 3D tracking/matchmoving, matte painting, particle systems, dynamics/destruction, stereoscopic 3D.
- [[10-Library/sources/s047-guiltygear-bone-placement-tips]] — *Bone Placement Tips for Action* (ArcSys dev document), Motomura (~2018). Guilty Gear Xrd rigging: hip/knee/ankle/toe bone placement guidelines for fighting game extremes; why bad bone placement can't be fixed by skinning; auxiliary bones.
- [[10-Library/sources/s048-guiltygear-modeling-for-skinning]] — *Modeling for Skinning* (ArcSys dev document), Motomura (~2018). Guilty Gear Xrd topology: the "50% Trap" (why joint skinning thins — linear blend skinning mathematics), "Tuck-in" and "C" structure solutions, modeling while thinking of joints.
- [[10-Library/sources/s049-juan-ordoniez-nivel-1-proceso]] — *Proceso de level design y relación entre departamentos*, Juan P. Ordóñez (ESNE lecture). Level designer as integrator; whitebox-first workflow; Agile/Scrum applied to game dev; department dependencies.
- [[10-Library/sources/s050-juan-ordoniez-4-flows]] — *Level Design: Los 4 Flows*, Juan P. Ordóñez (ESNE lecture). Four-flow documentation framework: Interactive, Narrative, Emotional, Spatial flows — align all four before building any level.
- [[10-Library/sources/s051-juan-ordoniez-vertical-slice]] — *Vertical Slice*, Juan P. Ordóñez (ESNE lecture). VS as a level design documentation method: first-person narrative, color-coded annotation (7 element types), production lists, team alignment.
- [[10-Library/sources/s052-mecanicas-de-juegos]] — *Mecánicas de Juegos (Corregido)* (author unattributed). Spanish-language game mechanics reference — **image-only PDF, no text extractable; requires OCR**. 6 pages, ~47 MB.
- [[10-Library/sources/s053-guidelines-hci-2015]] — *Guidelines for the Design of Movement-Based Games*, Mueller & Isbister (2015). 10 practitioner-validated guidelines in 3 clusters: movement requires special feedback; movement leads to bodily challenges; movement emphasizes certain kinds of fun. Relevant to any embodied/kinesthetic HCI.
- [[10-Library/sources/s054-casual-gaming-trends-2022]] — *Casual Gaming Trends Snapshot Report, March 2022*, GameRefiney. US iOS casual market data: meta-element adoption (70% Match3 have meta), renovation/construction trend (7%→49%), social/competitive feature creep, post-IDFA new-game challenges.
